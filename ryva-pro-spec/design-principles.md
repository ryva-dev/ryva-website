# Design Principles

## 1. What matters now

Every primary workspace begins with decisions, risks, and next actions. Historical detail remains accessible but does not dominate the first view.

## 2. Evidence adjacent to decisions

Claims, qualifications, suggestions, forecasts, and state changes expose source, date, confidence, missing information, and human owner without forcing navigation to an audit page.

## 3. Relationships over records

Records explain their place in the commercial relationship. A Product page shows Brand, evidence, readiness, possible Buyers, Opportunities, and outcomes. An order remains connected to the opportunity, account, agreement, and commission.

## 4. Honest state

The UI distinguishes:

- observed from inferred;
- proposed from approved;
- sent from drafted;
- estimated from ordered;
- earned from paid;
- active from stale;
- qualified from merely discovered.

## 5. One primary action

Pages may contain extensive information, but the main action area displays one recommended next action and its prerequisites. Secondary actions are visually quieter.

## 6. Spacious analytical density

Use compact tables, clear typography, predictable columns, expandable evidence, and disciplined whitespace. Avoid decorative card grids and oversized empty areas that reduce scan speed.

## 7. Progressive disclosure

Show summary, exception, and next action first. Reveal full evidence, field history, formula lineage, and audit details on demand.

## 8. Human decision visibility

AI suggestions have a distinct visual treatment, evidence panel, and Accept, Edit, Reject controls. Accepted AI text becomes user-owned only after explicit review.

## 9. No gamification

No streaks, points, confetti, leaderboards, motivational slogans, or badges unrelated to credential or verified status.

## 10. Accessible and keyboard-efficient

Meet WCAG 2.2 AA. Support keyboard navigation, visible focus, reduced motion, screen-reader labels, accessible charts, non-color status indicators, and comfortable target sizes.

## 11. Responsive continuity

Mobile supports time-sensitive execution—lookup, tasks, notes, calls, approvals, stage updates, commission review. Dense research, comparison, bulk import, sequence building, and analytics configuration remain desktop-first.

## Visual direction

- premium neutral base;
- restrained color for state and risk;
- no “boss babe,” influencer, social-feed, or chat-first aesthetic;
- tabular numbers for commercial values;
- clear provenance and confidence chips;
- persistent record identity and next action;
- motion only for state confirmation and spatial continuity.

## Component principles

- tables support column control, pinned identifiers, bulk selection, and saved views;
- cards are reserved for summary, exception, and Kanban, not every field;
- drawers support evidence, history, and quick-create;
- destructive or external actions require clear confirmation;
- empty states name the professional outcome and next setup action;
- errors preserve user input and explain recovery;
- loading states keep layout stable and expose partial data honestly.
