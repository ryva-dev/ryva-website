# Onboarding

## Objective

Onboarding teaches Ryva Pro through real setup. It ends with a valid professional workspace and one responsible next action, not a generic product tour.

## Preconditions

- authenticated and email-verified user;
- active eligible certification verified;
- subscription active or trial authorized;
- current terms accepted.

## Journey

### 1. Access confirmation

Show certification title, credential status, expiration, subscription, and permitted role. Resolve mismatches before collecting operational data.

### 2. Profile

Collect:

- professional name;
- time zone;
- preferred currency;
- work schedule and notification windows;
- contact identity used for outreach;
- category and Business-type experience;
- geographic and channel preferences.

### 3. Experience assessment

Self-assessment calibrates guidance density, not capability or access. Capture:

- years and types of relevant experience;
- comfort with wholesale terms;
- outreach experience;
- current portfolio size;
- training needs.

Label as user assertion.

### 4. Existing relationships

Choose:

- starting new;
- already represents Brands;
- already manages Business accounts;
- importing both.

Existing representation requires agreement/authority evidence or a visible pending-verification status.

### 5. Communication preferences

Configure:

- email integration;
- send-from identity;
- signature;
- permitted working hours;
- default follow-up reminder;
- opt-out and compliance acknowledgement.

Connection does not enable automatic sending.

### 6. Data import

Optional CSV/contact import for:

- Brands;
- Products;
- Businesses;
- Contacts;
- Opportunities;
- accounts;
- historical orders;
- commissions.

The user maps, validates, previews, resolves duplicates, and confirms before commit.

### 7. First Product

Save a Product from a URL, manual entry, or import. Capture source, preliminary evidence, unknowns, and Discover state.

### 8. First Brand

Create or link the Brand, verify basic identity, and record representation status.

### 9. First Business

Create or import a Business and at least one source-backed fit hypothesis. A Contact is optional until known.

### 10. First Opportunity

Create only if representation authority exists and the Opportunity definition is met. Otherwise create a Representation Opportunity or Product/Business research link and explain the gate.

### 11. First outreach

Available only after:

- authority;
- Business qualification;
- Contact and channel;
- Prepared stage requirements;
- no blocking conflict;
- final human approval.

The user may create and approve a one-contact send or a small campaign. No external communication occurs merely to complete onboarding.

## Onboarding states

- not started;
- credential blocked;
- subscription blocked;
- in progress;
- import processing;
- setup complete;
- deferred setup with limited Home guidance.

## Progress behavior

Use a restrained checklist, not gamification. Each step may be deferred except access and terms. Progress is saved. Completion reflects usable setup, not percentage engagement.

## Home after onboarding

Show:

- next responsible setup action if data is incomplete;
- first evidence review;
- authority verification;
- Contact verification;
- approved outreach task;
- reorder or commission reconciliation for imported accounts.

## Error and recovery

- credential failure: remain on access check with support route;
- subscription failure: preserve profile draft;
- provider connection failure: continue with manual workflow;
- import failure: preserve mapping and row errors;
- duplicate conflict: require resolution before affected records commit;
- browser exit: resume exact step.

## Acceptance

- no user reaches production workspace without valid access;
- onboarding never forces an external send;
- imported facts retain source and origin;
- AI suggestions are labeled and reviewable;
- authority gates apply to first Opportunity and outreach;
- completion produces at least one real or explicitly deferred next action.
