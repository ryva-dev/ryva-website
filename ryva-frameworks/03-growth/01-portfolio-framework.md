# RF-09: The Ryva Portfolio Framework

## Purpose

The Portfolio Framework helps a Representative build and manage a serviceable, complementary, economically sound, and risk-aware portfolio of Brands and Business accounts.

A healthy portfolio is not defined by one universal number of Brands or accounts. It is defined by fit, capacity, concentration, relationship quality, recurring value, conflicts, and the Representative's ability to meet commitments.

## Use

Use when accepting or renewing a Brand mandate, assigning account effort, reviewing concentration and capacity, handling conflicts, replacing inactive Brands, and planning growth. Do not use to maximize logo count, justify unmanaged workload, or treat every inactive relationship as a failure.

## Inputs

- active, pending, conditional, inactive, suspended, and ended Brand mandates;
- Product and category coverage;
- account relationships, ownership, status, and value;
- actual and expected commission economics;
- reorder and support history;
- Representative capacity, competence, territory, and service load;
- category, Brand, account, and revenue concentration;
- conflicts, exclusivity, channel restrictions, and dependencies; and
- portfolio objectives and review horizon.

## Portfolio lenses

### Brand portfolio

Evaluate:

- complementary versus competing Brands;
- category concentration;
- Product overlap and differentiation;
- commission quality and payment reliability;
- Brand support burden;
- inventory and operational reliability;
- reorder quality;
- mandate clarity, territory, and conflicts;
- active versus inactive status; and
- long-term partnership quality.

### Account portfolio

Evaluate:

- account concentration;
- recurring versus one-time value;
- Buyer and relationship depth;
- account ownership and contact coverage;
- reorder quality and payment reliability;
- service and issue burden;
- category and geographic exposure;
- strategic influence and introductions;
- inactivity, risk, and retention; and
- expansion fit.

## Health dimensions

| Dimension | Healthy condition | Warning condition |
|---|---|---|
| Complementarity | Brands and Products create coherent options without misleading overlap | Frequent substitution, channel conflict, or Buyer confusion |
| Concentration | Dependence is understood and survivable under plausible loss | One Brand/account/category dominates without mitigation |
| Revenue quality | Actual commissions are clear, collectible, and linked to sustainable relationships | Revenue is projected, disputed, one-time, or costly to service |
| Reorder quality | Repeat activity is need-based, reliable, and operationally supported | Reorders require pressure, discounting, or unresolved issues |
| Capacity | Commitments fit available time, competence, geography, and support ability | Follow-up failure, stale records, late issues, or excessive switching |
| Relationship depth | Multiple appropriate contacts, context, trust, and documented commitments | Single-contact dependency or transactional contact only |
| Brand support burden | Work is proportionate to value and agreed role | Representative absorbs unpriced Brand operations |
| Conflict control | Accounts, territories, competing Products, and exclusivities are visible and managed | Ambiguous ownership, duplicate pursuit, or undisclosed overlap |
| Activity quality | Active status reflects current evidence and next action | Dormant records remain active to inflate portfolio size |
| Adaptability | Portfolio can change without damaging obligations or trust | Locked concentration, weak exit terms, or no replacement path |

## Method

1. Define review period, objectives, capacity, and all in-scope Brand and account records.
2. Separate historical actuals, current state, and potential.
3. Calculate concentration and workload using transparent measures.
4. Review every health dimension and identify dependencies.
5. Classify each Brand and account:
   - **Invest** — supported value and capacity justify increased attention;
   - **Maintain** — healthy relationship requiring ordinary stewardship;
   - **Repair** — credible value exists but a defined issue requires correction;
   - **Monitor** — limited activity or evidence; maintain bounded review;
   - **Exit / Replace** — no sustainable fit, authority, value, or capacity.
6. Resolve conflicts and ownership before new pursuit.
7. Create a capacity and diversification action plan.
8. Record human decisions, obligations, and review dates.

## Inactive Brand and replacement rule

A Brand should not be labeled inactive solely because no recent commission occurred. Inactivity considers mandate status, communication, Product availability, active Opportunities, support, and review date.

Replacement may be considered when:

- the mandate ends or no longer supports three-party value;
- Product or Brand readiness materially declines;
- support burden is structurally disproportionate;
- conflicts cannot be managed;
- the Brand becomes persistently unresponsive or unreliable;
- portfolio strategy changes within contractual obligations; or
- a better-fit Brand can be accepted without improper use of confidential information.

Exit must honor agreements, open commitments, confidentiality, account ownership, and relationship stewardship.

## Portfolio health indicators for Ryva Pro

- active versus conditional/inactive Brand count;
- active account and relationship count;
- Brand, account, category, and commission concentration;
- verified recurring versus one-time commission;
- reorder rate and reorder quality;
- average service effort and overdue commitments;
- unresolved issue burden;
- mandate, territory, and conflict coverage;
- relationship depth and single-contact dependency;
- Product/Brand readiness freshness;
- inactive record age and reason;
- payment reliability;
- capacity utilization by stage; and
- proportion of portfolio value that is historical, current, or potential.

No single indicator defines health.

## Output and action

The output is a portfolio condition summary, concentration risks, capacity state, conflict register, Brand/account classifications, and an action plan with owners and dates.

## Guardrails

- Do not publish one universal ideal number of Brands or accounts.
- More Brands may reduce reliability rather than diversify risk.
- Projected commission is not portfolio revenue.
- Diversification does not justify competing representation that violates mandate or trust.
- A high-value account does not excuse neglect of commitments to others.
- AI may calculate and flag; the Representative owns acceptance, exit, conflict, and relationship decisions.

## Implementation Across Ryva

### Certification

Teach capacity, concentration, complementarity, account ownership, conflicts, recurring value, and ethical exits. Assess a portfolio review requiring tradeoffs, not an arbitrary account target. Include portfolio rationale in the final project.

### Ryva Pro

Build linked Brand and account portfolios, concentration dashboards, capacity indicators, conflict alerts, inactivity reasons, mandate dates, actual versus potential value, and review workflows. Preserve exit and ownership decisions.

### Closing Program

Use portfolio context to choose preparation and follow-up effort, manage competing offers, protect accounts, plan reorders, and avoid overcommitment. Closing targets must not reward unhealthy concentration or poor-fit acceptance.

### Marketing

Ryva may describe disciplined portfolio management and diversification. It must not promise an ideal number of Brands, predictable recurring income, or guaranteed account ownership.
