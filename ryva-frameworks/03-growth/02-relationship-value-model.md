# RF-10: The Ryva Relationship Value Model

## Purpose

The Relationship Value Model evaluates long-term value beyond the opening order. It separates what a relationship has produced, what it supports now, and what it may produce in the future.

## Use

Use during Support, Reorder, Grow, portfolio review, resource allocation, and relationship-risk review. Do not use projections as actual value, reduce a relationship to revenue alone, or exploit trust for unsuitable expansion.

## Inputs

- verified orders, reorders, commissions, payments, returns, and adjustments;
- relationship start, duration, activity, and status;
- Product and location expansion;
- introductions and referrals;
- payment reliability;
- issue and support history;
- relationship depth and trust evidence;
- Brand stability and Business condition;
- portfolio and strategic fit; and
- potential opportunities, assumptions, probabilities, effort, and risks.

## Three value states

### Historical value

What has occurred in a defined past period:

- verified opening and reorder volume;
- verified earned and paid commission;
- actual relationship duration;
- actual Product/location expansion;
- verified introductions or referrals;
- actual returns, disputes, delays, and support effort; and
- documented relationship outcomes.

Historical value is not the same as lifetime revenue unless the full period and definition support that claim.

### Current value

What the active relationship supports now:

- current order or use cadence;
- current payment reliability;
- current trust and contact depth;
- open commitments and issue burden;
- current portfolio fit and conflict state;
- current Brand and Business stability; and
- current capacity required to steward it.

Current value may rise or fall without a new order.

### Potential future value

What may occur under stated assumptions:

- possible reorder frequency and order range;
- possible Product or location expansion;
- possible introductions or influence;
- expected longevity;
- expected support cost;
- Brand and Business stability scenarios; and
- portfolio effect.

Potential value must be labeled as estimate, range, scenario, or hypothesis. It is never added to historical value and displayed as actual.

## Value dimensions

| Dimension | Historical evidence | Current evidence | Potential evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Reorder frequency | Verified dates and intervals | Current replenishment state | Estimated cadence and basis |
| Average order value | Actual net order values under defined treatment | Current price and mix | Scenario range, not booked value |
| Account longevity | Observed active period | Current status and renewal conditions | Stability hypothesis |
| Product expansion | Products actually added | Active assortment/use | Qualified expansion ideas |
| Introduction opportunities | Verified introductions | Current network willingness | Potential referrals with no assumption of consent |
| Trust | Kept commitments, corrections, disclosures | Current access, candor, and issue handling | Expected effect only if conditions persist |
| Payment reliability | Actual timing, disputes, defaults | Open balance/status where authorized | Risk estimate |
| Support requirements | Actual time and issue history | Current burden and owners | Expected future service range |
| Strategic influence | Verified decision or market contribution | Current relevance | Hypothesized learning, visibility, or access |
| Referral potential | Actual relevant referrals | Expressed willingness | Estimated potential, not promised |
| Brand stability | Historical operating record | Current evidence | Scenario, not certainty |
| Portfolio fit | Historical cross-portfolio effect | Current complementarity/conflict | Expected future fit |

## Method

1. Define the relationship, perspective, review period, and permitted data.
2. Build separate Historical, Current, and Potential records.
3. Classify all evidence under RF-12.
4. Normalize actual monetary values only where definitions and rights permit.
5. Record nonfinancial value and cost without forcing unsupported currency conversion.
6. Identify support burden, risk, concentration, and opportunity cost.
7. Assign a qualitative condition to each state:
   - **Weak**
   - **Developing**
   - **Healthy**
   - **Strategic**
8. State next action: Repair, Maintain, Reorder Review, Grow, Monitor, or Exit.
9. Set review trigger and preserve changes over time.

The condition labels require approval and validation before consequential use.

## Optional projections

If Ryva displays a financial scenario, it must show:

- time horizon;
- low/base/high or another approved range;
- order, reorder, commission, churn, return, payment, and effort assumptions;
- source and confidence for each assumption;
- actual-to-date separately; and
- sensitivity to material variables.

Do not label a probability-weighted estimate as expected cash without explaining the method and uncertainty.

## Output

- Historical Value summary;
- Current Value condition;
- Potential Future Value scenario;
- confidence and missing evidence;
- support burden and risk;
- relationship health indicators; and
- recommended next action.

## Guardrails

- Opening-order size is not relationship value.
- Trust is evidenced by conduct, not converted into invented currency.
- Referrals and introductions are not assets owned by the Representative.
- Potential is not actual, booked, earned, or guaranteed.
- High historical value cannot cure a current ethics, trust, or fit failure.
- AI may summarize and model scenarios; humans own relationship and expansion decisions.

## Implementation Across Ryva

### Certification

Teach value-state separation, actual versus projected economics, support cost, trust evidence, and ethical growth. Assess candidates on a relationship with high historical revenue but declining current health.

### Ryva Pro

Store value states separately; define monetary fields; link orders, payments, returns, time, issues, contacts, and expansion records; show ranges and assumptions; provide freshness and health alerts; preserve snapshots and human actions.

### Closing Program

Use the model for follow-up, reorder timing, account planning, value reviews, expansion, and repair. Do not use potential value to pressure the opening order.

### Marketing

Ryva may discuss long-term relationship value beyond initial orders. It must not publish projected value as actual earnings or imply guaranteed reorders, longevity, or referrals.
