# RF-06: The Ryva Business Fit Score

## Purpose

The Business Fit Score evaluates how well a specific Product and Brand fit a specific Business. It prevents indiscriminate prospecting by requiring a documented match thesis before outreach.

Fit quality matters more than raw prospect volume because poor-fit outreach consumes Brand capacity, Buyer attention, and Representative trust while producing little durable value.

## Use

Use during Target, before Prepare, when assortment or Business conditions change, and before account expansion. Apply to retailers, hospitality businesses, salons, gyms, corporate buyers, distributors, subscription boxes, and other legitimate Business customers. Do not use a general Product or Brand score as a substitute.

## Inputs

Product and Brand evidence; Business model, customers, locations, positioning, assortment, economics, operations, space, geography, timing, authority, and needs; territory and conflict records; proposed order terms; and uncertainty.

## Dimensions

Rate each applicable dimension 1–5 using the Standard anchors; Unknown remains missing.

| Dimension | Decision question and evidence | Effect on fit |
|---|---|---|
| Customer overlap | Do the Business's actual or credibly defined customers overlap with the Product's supported users? Use customer, transaction, assortment, or location evidence; demographics alone are insufficient. | Foundational to demand relevance. |
| Category relevance | Does the Product belong in the Business's assortment, service, use case, or procurement purpose? | Low relevance normally ends pursuit. |
| Price compatibility | Does expected retail or use value fit the Business's established price architecture and customer expectation? | Misalignment increases resistance and discount risk. |
| Retailer margin potential | Are Business economics workable under current, complete terms? | Structural weakness is a stop or redesign condition. |
| Business positioning | Does the Product reinforce the Business's quality, values, brand, and customer promise without unsupported assumptions? | Protects reputation and coherent presentation. |
| Physical or operational fit | Can the Business display, store, use, train, handle, and support the Product? | Material mismatch may make fit impossible regardless of demand. |
| Geography | Are climate, culture, regulation, logistics, territory, and local demand relevant and manageable? | Defines scope; geography is not a demographic shortcut. |
| Buyer demand | Has an authorized Buyer expressed or evidenced a relevant need? Distinguish observed need from inferred interest. | Direct need strengthens priority; absence limits confidence. |
| Seasonality | Does timing align with buying windows, use periods, shelf life, and lead time? | Strong fit at the wrong time may be deferred, not rejected. |
| Existing assortment | Does the Product fill a gap, deepen a useful category, or duplicate an existing item without a rationale? | Supports match thesis and merchandising plan. |
| Competitive overlap | Are substitutes, exclusivities, channel conflicts, and cannibalization understood? | Unmanaged conflict may stop pursuit. |
| Minimum-order compatibility | Can the Business reasonably accept MOQ, case packs, cash commitment, and storage burden? | A mismatch is a commercial or operational stop. |
| Reorder potential | Is repeat activity credible for this Business based on use, customer, and historical evidence? | Influences relationship value; estimates remain labeled. |
| Ease of merchandising or use | Can staff and customers understand, display, demonstrate, or use the Product with reasonable support? | Affects adoption and support burden. |
| Relationship value | Can this relationship create legitimate long-term value beyond one order for all parties? | Applies RF-02 and RF-10 without labeling potential as actual. |

## Method

1. Define the specific Business, location or unit, Buyer context, Product, terms, and time window.
2. Confirm active representation authority and territory.
3. Gather direct Business evidence and classify proxies.
4. Rate all applicable dimensions and record Unknowns.
5. Apply stop conditions: illegitimate Business purpose, authority failure, territory/account conflict, structurally unworkable economics, or material operational incompatibility.
6. Write a match thesis explaining the strongest value, principal risk, and evidence needed next.
7. Assign fit classification using approved rules.
8. Define the next action and expiry date.

## Output classes

- **Poor Fit** — material mismatch or stop condition; do not approach.
- **Possible Fit** — credible hypothesis but important evidence, timing, or condition remains; research or qualify.
- **Strong Fit** — supported fit across material dimensions; prepare a tailored approach.
- **Priority Fit** — Strong Fit plus strong evidence, timely need, manageable effort, strategic relevance, and no unresolved material conflict; prioritize preparation.

These are qualitative rules in the first draft. A numeric score may support classification but does not determine it until validated thresholds are approved.

## Provisional calculation

`unweighted fit score = sum of known applicable ratings ÷ (5 × known applicable dimensions) × 100`

Display coverage and confidence. If critical dimensions are Unknown, the result cannot exceed Possible Fit regardless of total. Configurable weighting may later reflect Business type and category.

## Synthetic example

Synthetic Product C and Boutique D rate 4 or 5 on customer overlap, category, price, positioning, assortment, merchandising, and relationship value, but MOQ compatibility is 1 and the Brand will not split cases.

The numeric result may appear strong. The result is **Poor Fit under current terms** because the operational economics are not workable. Next action: ask the Brand whether an authorized lower-MOQ test exists; do not pitch the Buyer first.

## Guardrails

- Do not score a generic “retail market”; name the Business context.
- Do not infer demand solely from customer demographics or social followers.
- A prestigious account is not automatically a Priority Fit.
- Prospect count, contactability, and Buyer seniority are not fit dimensions.
- AI may identify candidates and assemble evidence; the Representative owns qualification, match thesis, and outreach decision.

## Implementation Across Ryva

### Certification

Teach Business types, match-thesis construction, economics, operational fit, and ethical qualification. Assess candidates on rejecting high-status poor-fit prospects and tailoring a supported approach. Require one complete fit record in the final project.

### Ryva Pro

Implement a Product–Brand–Business–context record with dimension ratings, evidence, conflicts, territory, confidence, match thesis, class, expiry, and human approval. Use fit to rank research and preparation, not to auto-send.

### Closing Program

Use the fit record to prepare discovery, value presentation, objections, merchandising, order design, and follow-up. Poor fit must not be reframed as a closing challenge.

### Marketing

Ryva may explain that fit is evaluated Business by Business. It must not claim that a fit class guarantees Buyer interest, order, sell-through, or reorder.

## Validation

Test across all named Business types, single and multi-location contexts, seasonal Products, distributors, and non-retail procurement. Measure false-positive outreach and accepted-conversation quality.
