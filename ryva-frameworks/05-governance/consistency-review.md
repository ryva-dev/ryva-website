# Consistency Review

## Review scope

This review compares all 13 Frameworks, the terminology crosswalk, and implementation matrices against The Ryva Standard Version 1.0.

## Overall conclusion

The first draft is structurally consistent with The Ryva Standard. No governing Standard file was changed. No framework authorizes commission-first conduct, treats AI as the owner of relationship decisions, or presents scores as guarantees.

Formal adoption is not recommended until the decisions and validation work in the [Founder Decision Log](founder-decision-log.md) are complete.

## Lifecycle reconciliation

The proposed 12-stage Placement Cycle initially appeared to conflict with the Standard's 11-step lifecycle. The conflict was resolved by mapping:

- Discover and Target to research and match;
- Represent to define mandate;
- Approach and Present to engage;
- Place to facilitate and document;
- Support, Reorder, and Grow to steward and review.

The Standard lifecycle remains governing. RF-01 supplies an operational view for Certification and Ryva Pro. Formal adoption of the stage names remains a founder decision.

## Duplication review

### Product Score versus Retail Readiness

Product Score contains a Retail Readiness dimension because readiness affects investigation merit. RF-07 owns the readiness decision and detailed checklist. Product Score must reference its result rather than recreate the checklist in software.

### Product Score versus Brand Score

Product Score contains Brand Trust only to capture Product reliance on Brand conduct. RF-05 owns the Brand suitability decision. Software should link the Brand result and avoid copying its component ratings into RF-04.

### Business Fit versus Opportunity Score

RF-06 owns context-specific fit. RF-08 owns work priority after an Opportunity exists and adds authority, value, effort, strategic portfolio fit, and conflict. Opportunity Score must reference Business Fit rather than recompute it.

### Relationship Triangle versus Relationship Value

RF-02 is a decision gate for legitimate three-party value. RF-10 is a longitudinal model separating historical, current, and potential value. Neither replaces the other.

### Standard of Recommendation versus component scores

RF-03 integrates component evidence and ethical independence into an advocacy decision. It cannot be calculated as an average of RF-04, RF-05, RF-06, or RF-07.

### Decision Filter versus Standard decision framework

RF-11 operationalizes the Standard's existing decision classes, sequence, tests, and conflict order. It does not replace the governing decision framework or approval hierarchy.

No frameworks were consolidated because each retained a distinct decision purpose. Overlap is managed through references and ownership rules.

## Terminology conflicts

| Term | Conflict | Resolution in draft |
|---|---|---|
| Brand Placement Closer | Narrows the established Representative role to a transaction | Not adopted |
| Protected Account | Implies a right not established in the Standard | Marked unresolved; no protection assumed |
| Placement Opportunity | May duplicate Opportunity | Treated only as contextual label |
| Placement Value | May blur actual and potential value | Not adopted; use Relationship Value or estimated account value |
| Closing Program | Standard establishes Advanced Sales Program, not this name | Marked unresolved |
| Buyer Intelligence | Not defined; potential privacy and purpose ambiguity | Use specific Business/Buyer evidence pending decision |
| Commission Intelligence | Could encourage commission-first ranking | Limited proposed meaning and explicit prohibition |
| Qualified Product/Brand/Business | “Qualified” may be mistaken for universal approval | Requires context, evidence, date, and reviewer |

## Score purpose confirmation

| Score | Real decision purpose | What it must not decide |
|---|---|---|
| Product Score | Whether the Product deserves further investigation | Representation, legal approval, Buyer fit, revenue |
| Brand Score | Whether the Brand is suitable for representation consideration | Product merit, representation authority, creditworthiness |
| Business Fit Score | Whether a specific offer fits a specific Business | Product quality in general, Buyer intent, guaranteed order |
| Opportunity Score | Priority and next action for a defined Opportunity | Actual revenue, guaranteed opening, final recommendation |
| Relationship Value Model | Stewardship and growth action across time states | Guaranteed lifetime value or ownership of referrals |

Retail Readiness is a categorical standard, not primarily a numeric score. Portfolio health is a multidimensional review, not one aggregate total. Relationship Triangle and Recommendation are gated decisions.

## Commission review

Commission is included only as:

- legitimate Representative value in RF-02;
- an independence and conflict input in RF-03;
- a commercial dimension in RF-05 and RF-08;
- actual or potential economics in RF-09 and RF-10; and
- a possible distorting incentive in RF-11.

Every applicable framework states that commission cannot override fit, trust, authority, risk, or three-party value.

## AI review

AI is permitted to collect authorized candidate evidence, summarize, compare, detect patterns, prioritize research, prepare contacts, draft, remind, calculate approved formulas, and explain inputs. Human review remains central to recommendations, trust, fit, representation, claims, outreach, negotiation, closing, relationships, ethics, disputes, and overrides.

No Framework permits AI to fabricate missing data, create authority, silently change consequential status, or issue final Certification or disciplinary decisions.

## Contradictions discovered

1. The wider workspace contains AI-worker Ryva materials that conflict with the Brand Placement company identity. They were excluded, consistent with The Ryva Standard.
2. The brief's “Brand Placement Closer” conflicts with the broader Representative role. It was not adopted.
3. “Protected Account” implies an unapproved ownership/protection policy. It was not operationalized.
4. “Closing Program” is not the same established term as Advanced Sales Program. Its relationship remains unresolved.
5. The request for score outputs cannot establish approved bands because the Standard explicitly leaves weights and bands unresolved. Provisional calculations are therefore unweighted, and qualitative outputs remain subject to approval.

## Validation required before adoption

All frameworks require usability and comprehension testing. The highest-priority methodological validation applies to:

- RF-04 Product Score;
- RF-05 Brand Score;
- RF-06 Business Fit Score;
- RF-08 Opportunity Score;
- RF-10 Relationship Value projections;
- RF-12 Evidence confidence labels; and
- RF-13 AI review and automation controls.

Testing should include content validity, reviewer agreement, category and Business-type variation, emerging-Brand bias, missing data, gaming, adverse-evidence retention, outcome calibration, error severity, user behavior, and three-party effects.

## Final consistency controls

- Every score has one named decision purpose.
- All frameworks use the same Placement Cycle.
- No framework rewards commission over fit.
- AI remains an assistant.
- Actual, current, and potential values remain separate.
- Unknown is never averaged.
- Gates override totals.
- Marketing claims remain narrower than internal analysis.
- Formal adoption requires founder decisions, specialist review where relevant, versioning, and effective date.
