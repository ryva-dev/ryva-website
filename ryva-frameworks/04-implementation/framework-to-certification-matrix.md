# Framework-to-Certification Matrix

This matrix implements the competency and curriculum principles in The Ryva Standard. It does not set assessment weights or pass rules.

| Framework | Learning objectives and lessons | Assessment and practical exercise | Final certification project evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| RF-01 Placement Cycle | Explain stages, gates, backward movement, CRM evidence, and human decisions | Diagnose stage and next action in cases; operate a simulated lifecycle | Complete case record from Discover to defensible next action |
| RF-02 Relationship Triangle | Identify value, obligations, risks, and warning signs for three parties | Reject or condition a commission-attractive but one-sided placement | Three-party condition and evidence rationale |
| RF-03 Standard of Recommendation | Apply independence, stop, caution, and proceed tests | Defend Do Not Represent / Proceed With Caution / Qualified result | Signed recommendation record with scope and limitations |
| RF-04 Product Score | Evaluate 17 Product dimensions, evidence, coverage, and TikTok limits | Score a synthetic Product and challenge weak proxies | Product evaluation and next-investigation action |
| RF-05 Brand Score | Separate Brand from Product; evaluate readiness, trust, and red flags | Evaluate strong Product/weak Brand case | Brand evaluation, red-flag disposition, missing evidence |
| RF-06 Business Fit Score | Build context-specific match thesis across Business types | Compare poor, possible, strong, and priority fit | Named Business Fit record and tailored rationale |
| RF-07 Retail Readiness | Audit required, preferred, category-specific, and unknown elements | Conduct a readiness audit with conditional scope | Readiness outcome, conditions, and Buyer implications |
| RF-08 Opportunity Score | Combine distinct inputs; estimate value/effort; prioritize ethically | Rank Opportunities where commission and fit conflict | Priority, risks, estimate assumptions, next action |
| RF-09 Portfolio Framework | Manage complementarity, concentration, capacity, conflicts, and exits | Repair an overloaded or concentrated portfolio | Portfolio health review and action plan |
| RF-10 Relationship Value | Separate historical, current, and potential value | Analyze high historical value with declining current health | Three-state value record and stewardship action |
| RF-11 Decision Filter | Apply fast/full review, incentives, alternatives, and escalation | Review product, curriculum, and commercial decisions | Material decision record |
| RF-12 Evidence Standard | Classify evidence and set bounded confidence | Distinguish direct evidence, TikTok proxies, estimates, inference, unknown | Evidence register and substantiated claims |
| RF-13 Human Judgment | Allocate AI assistance and meaningful review | Design a safe AI-assisted workflow and identify prohibited delegation | AI-use disclosure, approvals, corrections, audit plan |

## Blueprint constraints

- RF-02, RF-03, RF-11, RF-12, and RF-13 contain non-compensatory ethical or judgment content, subject to formal assessment approval.
- Applied judgment must not be assessed solely through recall.
- Candidates must encounter missing, contradictory, and adverse evidence.
- Synthetic cases must be labeled and must not be represented as current market facts.
- Scoring arithmetic is secondary to evidence treatment, gates, interpretation, and next action.
