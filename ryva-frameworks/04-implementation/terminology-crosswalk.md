# Terminology Crosswalk

## Purpose

This crosswalk aligns common sales and CRM language with The Ryva Standard. Ryva terminology is used only where it adds precision. Familiar industry language remains appropriate when the Ryva alternative would be less clear.

The governing definitions are in [The Ryva Standard glossary](../../the-ryva-standard/07-reference/01-terminology-and-glossary.md).

## Crosswalk

| Generic or proposed term | Approved Ryva usage | Status and rule |
|---|---|---|
| Wholesale representation / independent sales representation | Brand Placement | Approved professional term. Do not use for advertising product placement. |
| Independent sales rep / manufacturer's rep | Brand Placement Representative / Representative | Approved. Does not determine legal contractor or agency status. |
| Brand Placement Closer | Brand Placement Representative | **Not adopted.** “Closer” narrows the role to a transaction and conflicts with stewardship. It may describe a Closing Program skill, not a credential or role, pending founder decision. |
| Sales process / pipeline | Placement Cycle | Approved within the Frameworks as the 12-stage execution model, subject to formal adoption. Pipeline remains acceptable for the CRM summary view. |
| Lead | Research candidate or contact | Use the precise object. A contact is not automatically a Business or Opportunity. |
| Prospect | Candidate Business | Use only before qualification. |
| Qualified lead / qualified prospect | Qualified Business | A Business meeting the Standard's fit, purpose, authority, capacity, and conduct conditions. Qualification is contextual. |
| Qualified product | Qualified Product | Framework term: a Product that passed a defined qualification review for a stated context. Not legal or regulatory clearance. |
| Qualified brand | Qualified Brand | Framework term: a Brand that passed a defined qualification review for a stated context. Does not create representation authority. |
| Deal / sales opportunity | Opportunity | Approved Standard term with defined minimum conditions. |
| Placement Opportunity | Opportunity | Acceptable contextual label for a Product–Brand–Business placement case; it does not create a separate object. |
| Representation Opportunity | Proposed Brand mandate / representation candidate | Use the clearer phrase until formally defined. Do not confuse with an Opportunity, which requires a potential Brand–Business relationship. |
| Fit / lead score | Business Fit / Business Fit Score | Use for a specific Product–Brand–Business context. |
| Sale / win | Placement or verified opening order | Use **Placement** for the commercial relationship and **opening order** for the first verified order. Do not use interchangeably. |
| Placement | Placement | Approved: a Brand–Business commercial relationship developed through Brand Placement. Does not imply guaranteed order or ongoing result. |
| Opening Order | Opening order | Accepted descriptive term for the first verified order in a relationship. It is not currently a capitalized Standard term. |
| Repeat order | Reorder | Accepted industry term. Must refer to a verified subsequent order, not a forecast. |
| Account | Business relationship / account | “Account” remains useful CRM language. Specify the Business and relationship; do not imply ownership. |
| Protected Account | Account subject to an approved protection or ownership rule | **Unresolved.** No protection right exists until policy, authority, scope, expiry, conflict, and appeal are approved. |
| Book of business | Brand portfolio and Account portfolio | Use separately where the distinction matters. |
| Brand Portfolio | Brand portfolio | Framework term for represented or evaluated Brands and their portfolio effects. |
| Account Portfolio | Account portfolio | Framework term for Business relationships managed by a Representative. |
| Customer lifetime value | Relationship Value | Use only through RF-10, which separates Historical, Current, and Potential Future Value. |
| Relationship Value | Relationship Value | Framework term for the multidimensional value and cost of an ongoing relationship. |
| Placement Value | Relationship Value or Opportunity value estimate | **Do not adopt without definition.** It can confuse actual placement outcomes with projected opportunity value. |
| Deal size / expected revenue | Estimated account value | Must state perspective, horizon, range, assumptions, and confidence. Never present as actual. |
| Lead priority / deal priority | Opportunity Priority | Framework term: the approved work priority for a defined Opportunity. |
| Shelf-ready / wholesale-ready | Retail Readiness | Framework term for operational readiness in a defined Business context. Not legal approval. |
| Product data / market research | Product Intelligence | Approved Standard concept: evidence about Product potential, readiness, fit, and risk. |
| Vendor intelligence / supplier data | Brand Intelligence | Approved Standard concept: evidence about Brand readiness, support, trust, and risk. |
| Account intelligence / prospect intelligence | Business and Buyer evidence; proposed Buyer Intelligence | **Buyer Intelligence unresolved.** Use specific Business, Buyer, authority, need, and relationship fields until defined. |
| Commission analytics | Commission Intelligence | **Proposed term.** May describe transparent commission basis, timing, risk, actuals, and estimates; must not become commission-first ranking. |
| Close / closed-won | Commercial commitment / verified placement | Use the evidence-specific state. “Closed-won” may remain a reporting alias if its evidence definition is explicit. |
| Account management | Relationship stewardship | Approved Standard term for ongoing support and review. “Account management” remains acceptable in familiar interfaces. |
| Nurture | Follow-up or relationship development | Prefer the actual action; avoid vague stages. |
| Churn | Relationship ended / inactive / lost, with reason | Use a reasoned status rather than an unexplained aggregate label where individual action is required. |
| Sales training | Advanced Sales Program or proposed Closing Program | Advanced Sales Program is established. “Closing Program” is introduced by this brief and requires founder definition. |

## Naming rules

1. Do not create a Ryva term when the industry term is clearer and does not conflict with the Standard.
2. Capitalization does not create policy, authority, ownership, or certification status.
3. “Qualified” always requires a stated object, context, criteria, evidence date, and reviewer.
4. “Protected” must not be used until the protection right is approved and enforceable.
5. “Value” must identify whether it is historical, current, or potential and whose perspective it represents.
6. “Intelligence” requires sourced, contextualized evidence; it is not a label for scraped data or AI output.
7. “Placement” refers to a relationship, not an impression, outreach, or forecast.
