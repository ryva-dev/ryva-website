| Framework | Preparation and discovery | Presentation, objections, negotiation, and closing | Follow-up and reorders |
|---|---|---|---|
| RF-01 Placement Cycle | Prepare from qualified stage evidence | Use Approach–Place exit criteria; do not skip readiness or authority | Apply Support–Reorder–Grow |
| RF-02 Relationship Triangle | Identify value and obligations for all parties | Resolve conditions without sacrificing one party | Recheck value when issues or economics change |
| RF-03 Recommendation | Confirm current recommendation and permitted claims | Withdraw or qualify unsupported advocacy | Reassess after material evidence changes |
| RF-04 Product Score | Learn Product proof, gaps, and risks | Present evidence without quoting internal score as proof | Use outcome evidence to improve evaluation |
| RF-05 Brand Score | Prepare accurate Brand capability and escalation | Do not promise operations beyond Brand evidence | Monitor support, payment, and reliability |
| RF-06 Business Fit | Build tailored discovery and match thesis | Relate value to actual Business needs; poor fit is not an objection | Reassess fit for expansion |
| RF-07 Retail Readiness | Confirm current terms, samples, logistics, and claims | Answer operational objections accurately; no close through a gate | Support handoff and readiness changes |
| RF-08 Opportunity Score | Allocate effort and timing | Do not let priority become pressure | Re-rank when evidence or effort changes |
| RF-09 Portfolio | Check conflicts, capacity, and account context | Manage competing offers and commitments | Protect stewardship capacity and plan exits |
| RF-10 Relationship Value | Understand actual/current relationship state | Do not sell potential as actual | Guide reorder, repair, maintenance, and growth |
| RF-11 Decision Filter | Test strategy and claims | Evaluate concessions, urgency, and exceptions | Review material change and public defensibility |
| RF-12 Evidence Standard | Build substantiated proof and honest unknowns | Label estimates, proxies, and inference | Convert verified outcomes into dated evidence |
| RF-13 Human Judgment | Use AI for research and draft options | Human owns discovery, final claims, negotiation, outreach, and close | AI reminds and summarizes; human owns relationship |

## Program design constraints

- Sales mastery must remain subordinate to recommendation quality and the Code of Ethics.
- Objection handling clarifies fit, evidence, terms, and choice; it does not neutralize legitimate risk through pressure.
- Closing is a documented commercial decision, not the whole profession.
- Follow-up, reorders, relationship repair, and responsible exit are assessed capabilities.
- AI-generated language must be verified and personally adopted before use.
- Program marketing must not imply guaranteed sales, income, or privileged assessment treatment.
