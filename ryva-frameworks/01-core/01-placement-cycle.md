# RF-01: The Ryva Placement Cycle

## Purpose

The Placement Cycle is the canonical execution model for Brand Placement. It answers: **What stage is this work in, what must be true now, and what evidence permits the next stage?**

It maps to the governing lifecycle in The Ryva Standard without replacing it:

| Standard lifecycle | Placement Cycle |
|---|---|
| Define mandate | Represent |
| Research | Discover, Target |
| Evaluate | Evaluate |
| Qualify | Qualify |
| Match | Target |
| Prepare | Prepare |
| Engage | Approach, Present |
| Facilitate | Place |
| Document | Every stage |
| Steward | Support, Reorder, Grow |
| Review | Evaluate, Support, Reorder, Grow |

## Use

Use the Cycle to manage one Product–Brand mandate through research, representation, buyer matching, placement, and recurring growth. Do not use it as proof of buyer intent, as a reason to skip gates, or as a linear activity quota. Work may move backward when evidence changes.

## Inputs

- Product and Brand records;
- representation mandate and authority;
- Product, Brand, and retail-readiness evaluations;
- Business and Buyer records;
- match thesis and Business Fit result;
- communications, commitments, terms, orders, and outcome evidence;
- relationship health and reorder evidence; and
- conflicts, restrictions, territory, capacity, and risk.

## Stage standard

### 1. Discover

**Purpose:** Identify Products and Brands that may merit structured evaluation.

**Entry:** A legitimate source or research question exists.

**Required activities:** Capture the Product, Brand, source, apparent use, possible buyer types, and reason for interest; screen obvious duplicates and prohibited sources.

**Required evidence:** Source, observation date, identity indicators, preliminary Product facts, and unresolved questions.

**Exit:** A research record exists and either advances to Evaluate or closes with a reason.

**Common failures:** Treating virality as demand; copying unverified claims; creating an Opportunity before a buyer or match thesis exists.

### 2. Evaluate

**Purpose:** Determine whether the Product and Brand deserve further investigation.

**Entry:** Minimum discovery record is complete.

**Required activities:** Apply the Product Score, Brand Score, Evidence Standard, and relevant risk screening; separate Product quality from Brand readiness.

**Required evidence:** Criterion-level evidence, missing information, confidence, warnings, and source rights.

**Exit:** Product and Brand are rejected, held for evidence, or advanced to Qualify.

**Common failures:** Averaging unknowns; allowing a strong Product to conceal a weak Brand; treating a score as approval.

### 3. Qualify

**Purpose:** Confirm the Product and Brand meet the minimum conditions for possible representation.

**Entry:** Evaluation supports continued diligence.

**Required activities:** Verify identity, authenticity, claims posture, commercial readiness, operational capacity, and material risks; apply the Standard of Recommendation provisionally.

**Required evidence:** Identity and authority evidence, risk disposition, Retail Readiness result, and documented open issues.

**Exit:** Product and Brand are each marked qualified, conditional, or not qualified for a defined context.

**Common failures:** Combining Product qualification with Brand qualification; confusing incomplete diligence with a pass; implying regulatory approval.

### 4. Represent

**Purpose:** Establish the Representative's actual mandate and operating authority.

**Entry:** Product and Brand qualification support representation consideration.

**Required activities:** Document Products, territory, channels, target Businesses, compensation, authority, exclusivity, restrictions, approved claims, operations, escalation, and duration.

**Required evidence:** Executed or otherwise approved mandate, authorized contacts, terms, materials, and conflict disclosures.

**Exit:** Representation is authorized and active, or declined, expired, suspended, or pending.

**Common failures:** Outreach before authority; implying power to bind; ignoring overlapping representation or territory conflicts.

### 5. Target

**Purpose:** Identify specific Businesses with a credible match thesis.

**Entry:** Active representation authority exists.

**Required activities:** Define an ideal Business profile; research Businesses and Buyers; apply Business Fit; check territory, existing accounts, conflicts, contact rights, and capacity.

**Required evidence:** Business facts, Buyer role or decision path, fit rationale, disqualifiers, and source dates.

**Exit:** A Business is closed as poor fit or becomes a candidate Placement Opportunity with a documented match thesis.

**Common failures:** Mass list building; targeting based on size alone; using irrelevant personal data; confusing a contact with a qualified Business.

### 6. Prepare

**Purpose:** Build an accurate, relevant approach plan for the specific Business.

**Entry:** Candidate Opportunity and sufficient Business Fit exist.

**Required activities:** Confirm purpose, channel, timing, value thesis, authorized claims, likely questions, evidence, samples, proposed next step, and contact preferences.

**Required evidence:** Approved materials, fit explanation, current terms, confidence and caveats, contact authority, and outreach review.

**Exit:** The Representative approves a specific approach or returns the Opportunity for more evidence.

**Common failures:** Generic scripts; unsupported personalization; stale terms; AI-drafted claims not verified by the Representative.

### 7. Approach

**Purpose:** Establish a relevant, truthful initial commercial conversation.

**Entry:** Approved preparation record and lawful, appropriate contact route.

**Required activities:** Identify role and represented interest; state a concise fit rationale; respect contact preferences; record response and next step.

**Required evidence:** Final communication, sender approval, send or meeting record, response, and opt-out status.

**Exit:** No engagement, deferred, disqualified, or a relevant conversation is accepted.

**Common failures:** Volume over fit; false familiarity; artificial urgency; stage advancement based only on message delivery.

### 8. Present

**Purpose:** Enable the Buyer to evaluate the Product and Brand on accurate commercial and operational information.

**Entry:** The Business has agreed to relevant evaluation.

**Required activities:** Conduct discovery; present the match thesis; clarify economics, readiness, operations, evidence, limitations, samples, and next decision.

**Required evidence:** Buyer needs, questions, objections, presented claims, materials, commercial assumptions, and agreed follow-up.

**Exit:** The Opportunity is declined, paused, requires further diligence, or proceeds to a commercial decision.

**Common failures:** Presenting before discovery; hiding limitations; treating polite interest as intent; making commitments outside authority.

### 9. Place

**Purpose:** Facilitate an informed, documented commercial commitment between the Brand and Business.

**Entry:** Fit and material terms have been evaluated by authorized parties.

**Required activities:** Confirm parties, Products, quantities or scope, prices, terms, responsibilities, ordering, fulfillment, returns, payment, and next steps; resolve or clearly identify open conditions.

**Required evidence:** Authorized order or agreement, accepted terms, dates, ownership, and operational handoff.

**Exit:** Verified opening order or other defined commercial commitment, or a recorded loss, withdrawal, or deferral.

**Common failures:** Calling a verbal expression a placement; recording estimated revenue as actual; concealing unresolved terms.

### 10. Support

**Purpose:** Protect implementation quality and relationship trust after commitment.

**Entry:** A verified placement or active commercial relationship exists.

**Required activities:** Monitor handoff, delivery, setup, issues, commitments, early feedback, payment state where authorized, and role boundaries.

**Required evidence:** Fulfillment and issue records, owner and due dates, Buyer and Brand feedback, and corrections.

**Exit:** Initial obligations are stable, the relationship closes, or it advances to reorder review.

**Common failures:** Disappearing after commission eligibility; assuming no complaint means success; accepting responsibility assigned to another party.

### 11. Reorder

**Purpose:** Determine whether repeat business is supported by actual need, performance, and relationship conditions.

**Entry:** The Business has sufficient experience with the Product or the agreed replenishment event occurs.

**Required activities:** Review sell-through or use evidence where available, inventory, timing, issues, economics, and Buyer preference; prepare an appropriate reorder action.

**Required evidence:** Historical orders, current stock or use signal, Buyer confirmation, issue status, and current terms.

**Exit:** Verified reorder, scheduled review, changed Product mix, or relationship closure.

**Common failures:** Pressuring a reorder without need; treating projected cadence as actual; ignoring returns or unresolved service issues.

### 12. Grow

**Purpose:** Expand a healthy relationship through evidence-supported Product, location, use-case, or introduction opportunities.

**Entry:** The existing relationship is in good standing and expansion would create credible three-party value.

**Required activities:** Apply the Relationship Value Model; identify expansion hypotheses; re-qualify fit and capacity; agree a measured next step.

**Required evidence:** Historical and current relationship value, support burden, expansion rationale, capacity, risks, and consent.

**Exit:** An approved expansion Opportunity enters Target or Prepare, or no growth action is warranted.

**Common failures:** Expanding to compensate for weak retention; confusing potential with historical value; exploiting trust to introduce poor-fit Products.

## CRM status mapping

| Stage | Canonical CRM status | Required system gate |
|---|---|---|
| Discover | Research candidate | Source and reason for investigation |
| Evaluate | Under evaluation | Separate Product and Brand records |
| Qualify | Qualification pending / conditional / complete | Risks and missing evidence visible |
| Represent | Mandate pending / active / inactive | Authority and effective dates |
| Target | Candidate Opportunity | Match thesis and Business record |
| Prepare | Approach in preparation | Business Fit and human approval |
| Approach | Contacted / response pending / engaged | Communication and consent record |
| Present | Buyer evaluating | Buyer needs, materials, and next step |
| Place | Commercial decision / committed | Verified authorization or outcome |
| Support | Implementation support | Commitments and issue ownership |
| Reorder | Reorder review / reordered | Historical versus proposed order |
| Grow | Expansion review | Relationship Value and renewed fit |

Statuses describe evidence. They must not be advanced to improve reporting.

## Output and action

The Cycle produces a current stage, evidence state, owner, next action, blockers, and review date. A stage change must be justified by its exit criteria. When evidence weakens, the correct action may be to move backward, pause, or close.

## Guardrails

- Product qualification, Brand qualification, representation authority, Buyer qualification, outreach, placement, account support, and recurring growth remain distinct.
- No stage creates legal authority.
- “Place” requires verified commercial commitment, not predicted revenue or polite interest.
- Contact volume is not cycle progress.
- AI may recommend a transition but may not silently change consequential status.
- Material overrides preserve the prior result and the human rationale.

## Implementation Across Ryva

### Certification

Use the Cycle as the organizing spine for learning objectives, stage-based lessons, scenario assessments, CRM simulations, and a final project that carries a synthetic case from Discover through a defensible next action. Candidates must demonstrate gates, evidence classification, backward movement, and ethical closure—not merely memorize stages.

### Ryva Pro

Implement distinct records, stage gates, required evidence, owners, timestamps, reason codes, backward transitions, alerts, and audit trails. Dashboards should show cycle distribution, time in evidence state, blockers, relationship outcomes, and false-progression indicators. AI may propose missing evidence and next actions; the system must capture human approval for material transitions.

### Closing Program

Organize sales preparation around Prepare through Place while teaching that closing depends on earlier qualification and later Support, Reorder, and Grow. Discovery, presentation, objections, negotiation, closing, and follow-up must map to evidence-based stage exits.

### Marketing

Ryva may describe the Cycle as an end-to-end professional method. It must not claim that completing the stages guarantees a placement, reorder, growth, or income. Stage names should not be promoted as legally protected status without approval.
