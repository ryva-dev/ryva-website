# RF-11: The Ryva Decision Filter

## Purpose

The Decision Filter gives Ryva employees, instructors, Representatives, and product teams one repeatable test for significant decisions. It determines whether a decision may proceed, needs revision, or requires escalation.

## Use

Use for material or unfamiliar choices affecting professional conduct, Certification, Product behavior, Brand or Business relationships, data, public claims, incentives, or the four pillars. Use the fast version for reversible, bounded choices. Use the full review for material or foundational decisions.

Do not use the Filter to bypass the approval hierarchy, replace specialist advice, or relabel a prohibited act as an acceptable tradeoff.

## Inputs

- proposed decision and intended outcome;
- affected stakeholders and Ryva pillars;
- governing provisions, authority, and constraints;
- facts, estimates, assumptions, unknowns, and contrary evidence;
- alternatives, risks, incentives, reversibility, and owner; and
- human and AI roles.

## Fast decision version

Ask five questions:

1. **Value:** Does this create legitimate value for the Brand, Business, and Representative?
2. **Trust:** Is it truthful, supportable, and consistent with the Standard?
3. **Fit:** Does it improve the profession or relationship rather than activity alone?
4. **Judgment:** Does it reduce unnecessary work without removing essential human responsibility?
5. **Defensibility:** Would Ryva be comfortable explaining the decision, evidence, and incentives publicly?

If every answer is supported and the decision is reversible, proceed with an owner and review condition. Any “no” requires revision or stop. Any material unknown requires full review.

## Full decision-review version

### 1. Define

- What outcome should change?
- For whom?
- Which decision is being made now, and which is not?

### 2. Establish authority

- Which Standard, policy, agreement, law, or permission governs?
- Who has the right and competence to decide?

### 3. Classify evidence

- Which inputs are verified facts, direct evidence, proxies, estimates, assumptions, model inferences, or unknowns?
- What evidence contradicts the preferred option?

### 4. Apply the ten tests

1. Does this create legitimate value for the Brand?
2. Does this create legitimate value for the Business Buyer?
3. Does this help the Representative operate more professionally?
4. Does it strengthen long-term relationships?
5. Is it supported by evidence proportionate to the claim?
6. Does it protect trust, truthful representation, and meaningful choice?
7. Does it reduce unnecessary work without removing essential human judgment?
8. Does it align with The Ryva Standard and the four pillars?
9. Would Ryva be comfortable defending the decision publicly?
10. Does it improve the profession rather than merely increase short-term revenue?

Record each as **Pass**, **Conditional**, **Fail**, or **Unknown**.

### 5. Examine incentives and failure

Identify how commission, revenue, completion, engagement, speed, ranking, or executive preference could distort behavior. Define the most credible failure mode and who bears its cost.

### 6. Compare alternatives

Include doing nothing and a smaller reversible option. State why the selected option is superior under the governing priorities.

### 7. Decide

Outputs are:

- **Proceed** — all material tests pass.
- **Proceed With Conditions** — no material test fails; explicit conditions, owner, and review trigger control remaining risk.
- **Revise** — the intended outcome may be valid, but the method fails or lacks evidence.
- **Do Not Proceed** — a material test fails or the action violates authority, integrity, or trust.
- **Escalate** — the decision exceeds authority, competence, evidence, or approved risk.

## Escalation criteria

Escalate when a decision:

- may conflict with law, regulation, the Standard, or a binding agreement;
- changes certification claims, ethics, designation, access, or assessment;
- introduces a new consequential score, gate, weight, or public ranking;
- permits external AI action or changes meaningful human review;
- uses personal, confidential, licensed, or cross-customer data in a new way;
- creates an unmanaged conflict among Ryva's commercial interest and fit;
- is difficult to reverse and could materially affect trust or reputation; or
- lacks a decision owner with appropriate authority.

## Examples

### Reversible product feature

Proposal: show a reminder when Product evidence is stale. The reminder strengthens evidence quality, does not change a score, and can be dismissed with a reason. **Result: Proceed** with measurement of useful corrections.

### Commission-ranked Opportunity list

Proposal: rank Opportunities primarily by commission. Brand and Business value may be displaced; incentives are opaque; the approach conflicts with trust before commission. **Result: Do Not Proceed.** Revise ranking around fit, confidence, strategic value, and sustainable economics.

### AI outreach

Proposal: let AI send first outreach automatically. The Standard requires approved human control for material relationship communications, and the exact automation policy is unresolved. **Result: Escalate** and retain human approval.

### Certification pass-score change

Proposal: lower the pass score to increase completion. Certification credibility and assessment validity are affected; commercial outcomes cannot control results. **Result: Do Not Proceed** without evidence, psychometric review, and foundational approval.

## Guardrails

- A majority of passes cannot override one material fail.
- Public defensibility does not mean popularity; it means a truthful explanation can withstand scrutiny.
- The Filter records reasoning; it does not grant authority.
- AI may organize inputs and identify conflicts, but an accountable person owns the decision.

## Implementation Across Ryva

### Certification

Teach the fast and full versions in ethics, professional judgment, and ownership. Assess candidates on ambiguous cases and require a decision record in the final project.

### Ryva Pro

Provide a lightweight fast check and a full decision record with evidence links, affected parties, conditions, approvals, review dates, and immutable history. Trigger escalation workflows automatically from defined criteria.

### Closing Program

Use the Filter before material claims, concessions, negotiated exceptions, pressure tactics, and account expansion. Objection handling must not turn a failed trust test into a sales technique.

### Marketing

Apply the full review to certification, income, Product, score, AI, and network claims. Marketing may describe the Filter but must not imply that it replaces legal or specialist review.
