# RF-13: The Ryva Human Judgment Standard

## Purpose

The Human Judgment Standard defines where AI and automation may assist and where meaningful human review remains mandatory. Technical ability to automate does not establish professional authority to automate.

## Use

Apply whenever Ryva Pro, a Representative, an instructor, or a product team proposes AI-supported analysis, workflow, communication, scoring, or action. Do not use “human in the loop” as a label without defining what the person sees, decides, and can change.

## Inputs

- task and intended outcome;
- affected parties and relationship consequence;
- evidence and data rights;
- reversibility, externality, sensitivity, and error severity;
- required competence and authority;
- proposed automation level; and
- review, correction, and audit controls.

## Assistance allocation

| Activity | AI role | Required human role |
|---|---|---|
| Data collection | Locate and structure authorized candidate evidence | Approve sources and verify material facts |
| Summarization | Condense records and highlight changes | Confirm meaning, omissions, and context |
| Pattern detection | Surface correlations, anomalies, and candidates | Decide relevance and avoid causal overclaim |
| Product comparison | Organize criteria and evidence | Determine suitability and limitations |
| Research prioritization | Rank investigation candidates | Choose priorities and stop conditions |
| Contact preparation | Research roles and assemble a brief | Confirm identity, relevance, channel, and purpose |
| Drafting | Produce options from approved facts | Own claims, tone, personalization, and final content |
| Follow-up reminders | Detect due or stale commitments | Decide whether and how to contact |
| Pipeline summaries | Summarize recorded state | Correct state and decide action |
| Score preparation | Calculate approved formula and flag missing data | Approve inputs, gates, override, and use |

## Decisions requiring meaningful human review

Human judgment remains central to:

- final Product recommendation;
- Brand legitimacy and trust decisions;
- Buyer qualification and Business Fit;
- acceptance, scope, and renewal of representation;
- match thesis and Opportunity priority;
- claims made to a Buyer;
- final outreach and material follow-up;
- relationship management;
- live discovery and presentation;
- negotiation, concessions, and closing;
- ethical decisions and conflict disposition;
- dispute, complaint, and issue resolution;
- account protection or ownership decisions;
- certification, ethics, or disciplinary outcomes; and
- exceptions to gates or approved policy.

“Meaningful” review requires the person to have the competence, time, evidence, authority, and interface needed to understand and change the result before consequence.

## Automation decision method

1. Define the exact task and side effect.
2. Identify whether it is internal or external, reversible or irreversible, and low or high consequence.
3. Confirm data authority and evidence quality.
4. Assign one approved level:
   - **Assist** — options or analysis only;
   - **Prepare** — a proposed artifact or record;
   - **Bounded internal execution** — approved internal state change under explicit rules;
   - **External execution** — action outside Ryva under explicit authority, review, and safeguards.
5. Define the human decision, required context, and approval timing.
6. Define refusal, escalation, correction, and stop behavior.
7. Record AI contribution, model or rule version, human decision, and outcome.
8. Evaluate errors and relationship effects before expanding autonomy.

External execution policy remains unresolved in The Ryva Standard.

## Stop and escalation conditions

Do not automate when:

- authority, identity, or data rights are unclear;
- the system cannot show material evidence and limitations;
- the human review is nominal or occurs only after irreversible effect;
- an error could materially mislead a Buyer or damage a relationship;
- the task requires empathy, negotiation, ethical interpretation, or dispute resolution beyond a bounded assistive role;
- model output would determine Certification or discipline without approved accountable review; or
- the system is optimized for volume at the expense of relevance, consent, or trust.

## Output and action

The output is an automation level, human decision point, required evidence, permissions, failure behavior, audit fields, and review condition. If these cannot be defined, the task remains manual or assistive.

## Guardrails

- AI never becomes the accountable Representative.
- Draft generation is not human authorship until reviewed and adopted.
- A confidence label does not transfer accountability.
- Automation must not conceal its contribution where disclosure is material.
- Users must be able to correct records and stop authorized automation.
- Learning across customers remains subject to approved data policy.

## Implementation Across Ryva

### Certification

Assess candidates on verification, data protection, false personalization, automation levels, and final-decision ownership. The final project must disclose AI use and show human corrections.

### Ryva Pro

Encode automation level, permission, data scope, human approval, idempotency where relevant, audit history, model version, refusal, and stop controls. AI recommendations must expose inputs, missing evidence, and correction routes.

### Closing Program

AI may support research, preparation, drafts, and reminders. The Representative owns discovery, claims, final outreach, objections, negotiation, closing, follow-up judgment, and relationship repair.

### Marketing

Describe AI as assistance to professional judgment. Do not imply autonomous relationship ownership, guaranteed decisions, or human work where no meaningful human review occurred.
