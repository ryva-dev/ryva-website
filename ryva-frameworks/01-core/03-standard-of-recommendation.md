# RF-03: The Ryva Standard of Recommendation

## Purpose

The Standard of Recommendation determines whether a Representative has a defensible basis to advocate for a Product or Brand. It answers: **May I put my professional judgment and relationship trust behind this recommendation?**

The independence question is:

> Would I recommend this opportunity if I received no commission?

A “yes” is necessary evidence of independence, not sufficient evidence of suitability.

## Use

Use before accepting representation, before first Buyer advocacy, when material evidence changes, and before expanding a recommendation into a new category or Business context. Do not use it as legal approval, regulatory clearance, a Product-safety certification, or a guarantee.

## Inputs

- Product Score and evidence;
- Brand Score and evidence;
- Retail Readiness result;
- relevant Business Fit when a specific Business is involved;
- claims, compliance, safety, returns, and reputation information;
- fulfillment and inventory evidence;
- commercial and margin analysis;
- mandate, authority, compensation, and conflicts; and
- missing evidence and confidence.

## Recommendation test

The Representative must evaluate:

| Test | Required question |
|---|---|
| Product quality | Is there credible evidence that the Product performs its intended function at an acceptable quality? |
| Review evidence | Are reviews credible, relevant, sufficiently contextual, and free of known manipulation? |
| Brand legitimacy | Is the Brand's identity, ownership, Product authenticity, and authority reasonably established? |
| Claims | Can material representations be supported and made within approved authority? |
| Buyer fit | Is there a credible fit for the intended Business type or named Business? |
| Commercial value | Can the relationship create legitimate value for all three parties? |
| Margin quality | Are the economics workable without misleading assumptions? |
| Fulfillment reliability | Can the Brand supply, ship, support, and correct problems at the proposed scale? |
| Return risk | Are expected return causes, process, economics, and responsibilities understood? |
| Reputational risk | Could association foreseeably damage the Business, Representative, or Ryva beyond an accepted and disclosed level? |
| Long-term potential | Is there a credible basis for stewardship, repeat business, or durable relationship value? |
| Independence | Would the Representative still regard the opportunity as suitable without commission? |

Each test is recorded as **Supported**, **Conditional**, **Unsupported**, or **Unknown**, with evidence under RF-12.

## Conditions

### Automatic stop conditions

Stop and return **Do Not Represent** when credible evidence establishes:

- fraudulent identity, evidence, reviews, authorization, or Product authenticity;
- unlawful Product or proposed claim;
- unresolved material safety risk;
- no authority to represent or communicate the proposed claim;
- known inability or refusal to fulfill material commitments;
- economics that depend on deception or concealed terms;
- a material conflict that cannot be disclosed or managed;
- pressure to fabricate demand, Buyer interest, scarcity, or outcomes; or
- another condition that makes advocacy incompatible with the Code of Ethics.

Potential legal or regulatory concerns require qualified review. This framework does not decide legality.

### Caution conditions

Return **Proceed With Caution** when no stop condition exists but:

- material evidence is missing, stale, indirect, or disputed;
- readiness is conditional;
- returns, complaints, inventory, fulfillment, or channel conflict require mitigation;
- a Product is promising but unproven in the intended context;
- margin or commission viability depends on unresolved assumptions;
- fit is limited to a narrow Business profile; or
- the relationship requires monitoring beyond ordinary practice.

The output must state the conditions, owner, evidence required, limits on advocacy, and review date.

### Proceed conditions

Return **Qualified to Represent** only when:

- no stop condition exists;
- every material test is Supported or an approved, bounded Conditional;
- identity, authority, and mandate are established;
- claims and commercial information are supportable;
- readiness is adequate for the intended context;
- the Relationship Triangle supports continued pursuit; and
- the Representative can explain the recommendation and its limitations.

## Method

1. Define the Product, Brand, category, channel, territory, and intended Buyer context.
2. Gather required inputs and classify evidence.
3. Apply stop conditions before considering aggregate strength.
4. Complete every recommendation test.
5. Apply the Relationship Triangle.
6. Record the independence answer and any incentive conflict.
7. Assign the most conservative output supported by the evidence.
8. State the next action, limits, owner, and review trigger.

No numeric average may convert an Unsupported material test into Qualified to Represent.

## Output

- **Do Not Represent**
- **Proceed With Caution**
- **Qualified to Represent**

The record must include scope, rationale, evidence confidence, critical risks, missing evidence, permitted claims, conditions, next action, owner, and review date.

## Guardrails

- Qualification is contextual and time-bound.
- The result does not imply Ryva endorsement unless Ryva has separately approved such a claim.
- A Product Score or Brand Score alone cannot produce Qualified to Represent.
- Commission, Brand fame, or social popularity cannot override a stop condition.
- The Representative—not an AI system—owns the final recommendation.

## Implementation Across Ryva

### Certification

Make recommendation judgment an Applied judgment outcome across ethics, Product and Brand evaluation, matching, analytics, and communication. Assess stop/caution/proceed distinctions and require candidates to defend evidence, limitations, and independence in the final project.

### Ryva Pro

Implement the test as a versioned review with criterion states, evidence links, gates, conditions, scope, expiry, human approver, and change history. AI may prefill evidence and flag contradictions but cannot issue the final result.

### Closing Program

Require a current recommendation result before presentation. Teach Representatives to qualify claims, disclose limitations, and withdraw advocacy when evidence changes. Closing technique cannot cure an unsupported recommendation.

### Marketing

Ryva may explain that Representatives use a formal recommendation test. It must not market Qualified to Represent as legal clearance, regulatory approval, guaranteed quality, or guaranteed commercial success.
