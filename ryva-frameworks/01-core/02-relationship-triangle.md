# RF-02: The Ryva Relationship Triangle

## Purpose

The Relationship Triangle tests whether a proposed or active placement creates legitimate, sustainable value for the Brand, Business, and Representative.

**A commission does not make a placement successful. Mutual commercial value does.**

The principle does not require identical value for each party or certainty of outcome. It requires informed participation, credible value, sustainable economics, and manageable obligations for all three.

## Use

Use before recommending, placing, renewing, expanding, or materially changing a relationship. Use again when evidence, economics, service burden, or trust changes. Do not use it to guarantee performance, suppress a legitimate negotiation, or rationalize poor fit because two parties benefit.

## Inputs

- Product, Brand, Business, and Opportunity evidence;
- mandate, authority, terms, compensation, and conflicts;
- Business Fit and Retail Readiness;
- operational capacity, risks, and open conditions;
- historical relationship and order evidence where available; and
- each party's stated objectives and responsibilities.

## Three-party model

| Party | Legitimate value | Obligations | Principal risks | Healthy indicators | Do-not-proceed indicators |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Brand | Suitable distribution; credible new relationships; accurate market feedback; repeatable growth | Accurate claims and terms; reliable supply and support; fair compensation; issue resolution | Poor-fit distribution; channel conflict; overextension; misrepresentation | Clear mandate; dependable fulfillment; responsive support; sustainable repeat business | Fraudulent identity or evidence; unresolved material safety/claims issue; inability or unwillingness to fulfill; authority unclear |
| Business Buyer | Suitable Product; viable economics; reliable operations; customer or business value; informed choice | Honest needs and authority; agreed payment and operational commitments; appropriate use of information | Poor sell-through or utility; returns; service burden; reputational harm; unsuitable economics | Product and price fit; clear decision path; workable operations; repeat demand; issues resolved | No legitimate business purpose; economics structurally unworkable; material needs unmet; decision-maker or authority fabricated |
| Representative | Fair compensation; durable professional relationships; portfolio quality; learning and reputation | Competent diligence; truthful communication; disclosure; records; follow-through; stewardship | Unpaid work; excessive support; conflict; reputational damage; authority overreach | Clear role; viable commission; manageable workload; trusted access; repeatable account value | Compensation depends on deception; mandate exceeds competence or capacity; undisclosed conflict; trust cannot be maintained |

## Decision test

For each party, answer:

1. What specific value is expected?
2. Which evidence supports that value?
3. Which value is only estimated or assumed?
4. What must the party contribute or accept?
5. Which downside could make participation unreasonable?
6. Is the economics of the relationship workable?
7. Is the party informed about material terms, limitations, and incentives?
8. What evidence would cause the Representative to stop?

Then assign one condition to each side:

- **Unsupported:** legitimate value is not established or a stop condition exists.
- **Conditional:** credible value exists but a material condition, unknown, or mitigation remains.
- **Supported:** direct or sufficiently strong evidence supports value and obligations are manageable.

The Triangle result is the weakest party condition:

- any Unsupported side → **Do Not Proceed**;
- no Unsupported side and at least one Conditional side → **Proceed Only Under Stated Conditions**;
- all three Supported → **Three-Party Value Supported**.

This is a structured decision, not a numeric score.

## Warning signs

- commission is the only clearly quantified benefit;
- the Business is expected to accept economics the Brand would not accept itself;
- the Brand depends on indiscriminate outreach or misleading claims;
- the Representative cannot explain the fit without popularity language;
- support responsibilities are vague or assigned to the party least able to perform them;
- one party's value depends on another party not understanding a term;
- early issues are hidden to preserve the relationship appearance; or
- expansion is proposed before the opening relationship is stable.

## Output and action

Record the condition of each side, evidence, unknowns, obligations, stop conditions, and next action. Conditional results require an owner and review date. A Supported result permits continued evaluation; it does not compel a placement.

## Guardrails

- The Triangle does not replace Product, Brand, Business Fit, Retail Readiness, or Opportunity evaluation.
- Equal monetary value is neither required nor assumed.
- Forecasts remain estimates.
- Representative compensation is legitimate value but cannot cure poor Brand or Business value.
- A human must decide whether disclosed risks are acceptable within authority.

## Implementation Across Ryva

### Certification

Teach three-party value in ethics, matching, commercial fundamentals, and relationship operations. Assess candidates with cases where commission is attractive but one party lacks legitimate value. The final project must state value, obligations, risks, and evidence for all three parties.

### Ryva Pro

Add party-specific value statements, evidence references, obligations, warnings, and condition labels to Opportunities. Surface a triangle warning when any side lacks evidence. Preserve who approved a conditional result and when it expires.

### Closing Program

Use the Triangle during discovery, presentation, negotiation, closing, follow-up, and reorders. Objection handling must clarify value and conditions, not pressure a weak side into acceptance.

### Marketing

Ryva may communicate its commitment to mutual commercial value. It must not claim every certified placement succeeds or that the Triangle predicts outcomes.
