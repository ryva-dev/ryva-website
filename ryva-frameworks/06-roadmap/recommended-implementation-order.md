# Recommended Order of Implementation

## Sequencing principle

Implementation should follow decision dependency, risk, and learning value. Software should not automate a framework before its terminology, evidence rules, human ownership, and validation requirements are stable.

## Phase 0: Governance and pilot design

1. Resolve FFD-001, FFD-021, FFD-023, FFD-026, FFD-040, and FFD-041.
2. Name owners for Frameworks, Certification, data, scoring, AI, and specialist review.
3. Select pilot categories, Business types, markets, and representative cases.
4. Approve version, change, evidence retention, and correction procedures.
5. Freeze the controlled terminology used in the pilot.

**Gate:** Authority, scope, participants, evidence rights, and acceptance criteria are documented.

## Phase 1: Evidence and human accountability

Implement:

1. RF-12 Evidence Standard;
2. RF-13 Human Judgment Standard; and
3. RF-11 Decision Filter.

Build the evidence record, classification, source lineage, dates, confidence, Unknown behavior, human decision fields, and audit history before numerical scores.

**Gate:** Reviewers classify evidence consistently; AI contribution and human approval are inspectable; material decisions have escalation.

## Phase 2: Lifecycle and relationship doctrine

Implement:

1. RF-01 Placement Cycle;
2. RF-02 Relationship Triangle; and
3. terminology required for stages and CRM records.

Pilot the stage gates and three-party test in curriculum cases and a minimal Ryva Pro workflow without automated outreach.

**Gate:** Users distinguish stages, move backward when evidence changes, and reject one-sided placements.

## Phase 3: Qualification foundations

Implement:

1. RF-07 Retail Readiness Standard;
2. RF-04 Product Score; and
3. RF-05 Brand Score.

Start with unweighted results, criterion evidence, coverage, confidence, and gates. Compare human reviewers before considering weights.

**Gate:** Product and Brand decisions remain separate; emerging Brands are treated fairly; stop conditions are reliably detected.

## Phase 4: Matching and recommendation

Implement:

1. RF-06 Business Fit Score; and
2. RF-03 Standard of Recommendation.

Use named Business cases across retailer, hospitality, salon, gym, corporate, distributor, subscription, and other contexts. Keep final recommendation human-owned.

**Gate:** Business Fit improves outreach relevance, and reviewers can defend stop/caution/proceed decisions.

## Phase 5: Opportunity prioritization

Implement RF-08 Opportunity Score only after source evaluations and mandate/territory/conflict records are reliable.

Begin with separate displays for fit, confidence, value estimate, effort, strategic value, commission, and risk. Do not collapse them into a production weighted score until validation supports it.

**Gate:** Priority improves qualified progression and workload focus without favoring commission or speculative revenue.

## Phase 6: Stewardship and portfolio

Implement:

1. RF-10 Relationship Value Model; and
2. RF-09 Portfolio Framework.

Start with historical and current records. Add future-value scenarios only after actual order, return, payment, effort, and relationship data definitions are stable.

**Gate:** Users distinguish actual from potential, surface concentration and workload risk, and make defensible maintain/repair/grow/exit decisions.

## Phase 7: Certification adoption

1. Finalize framework learning objectives and case library.
2. Validate assessment blueprint, rubrics, assessor training, and inter-rater consistency.
3. Pilot practical exercises and final project.
4. Review fairness, accessibility, security, and critical-fail rules.
5. Release only through controlled Certification versioning.

**Gate:** Assessment evidence supports the competency claim and is independent of commercial completion targets.

## Phase 8: Closing Program adoption

Resolve its relationship with the Advanced Sales Program. Then implement preparation, discovery, presentation, objections, negotiation, closing, follow-up, and reorders using the matrix.

**Gate:** Sales methods remain subordinate to recommendation quality, evidence, meaningful choice, and relationship stewardship.

## Phase 9: Public communication and scale

1. Approve public terminology and claim substantiation.
2. Publish only validated framework descriptions and qualified results.
3. Monitor gaming, bias, misuse, score drift, AI errors, and relationship outcomes.
4. Version models and migrate records transparently.
5. Expand categories and markets only after category-specific review.

**Gate:** Public claims do not exceed evidence, and scale does not weaken trust, Certification, or three-party value.

## First operational artifact recommended

The first build should be a non-public, human-reviewed evidence and lifecycle prototype containing:

- Evidence records and confidence;
- Product, Brand, Business, mandate, and Opportunity objects;
- Placement Cycle stages and gates;
- Relationship Triangle;
- Retail Readiness checklist;
- human approvals and audit history; and
- no automatic external outreach.

This prototype creates the data and reviewer evidence needed to validate later scores without prematurely presenting numerical precision.
