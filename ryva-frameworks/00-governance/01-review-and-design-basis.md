## Governing constraints

The Frameworks must preserve the following established principles:

1. Brand Placement creates sustainable value for the Brand, Business, and Representative.
2. Trust, integrity, truthful representation, certification credibility, and legal compliance take priority over commission or short-term revenue.
3. Product quality, Brand readiness, Business fit, and Opportunity quality are different decisions and must not be collapsed into one score.
4. A score is versioned decision support, not a guarantee, entitlement, scientific fact, or substitute for judgment.
5. Unknown is not neutral, average, or positive evidence.
6. Evidence must be classified, sourced, dated, and proportionate to the claim.
7. Material adverse evidence must remain visible.
8. Brand size, age, funding, or fame is not an automatic acceptance or rejection rule.
9. Pipeline state describes evidence; it does not prove buyer intent or commercial value.
10. The Representative retains accountability for recommendations, communications, and relationship judgment.
11. AI may assist analysis and preparation but must not fabricate evidence, impersonate a Representative, or remove meaningful human control.
12. Certification is private, competency-based, time-limited, and distinct from government licensing.
13. Frameworks, curricula, product specifications, and marketing are lower-order interpretations of the Standard.

## Established terminology

The Standard already defines Brand, Business, Business Buyer, Brand Placement, Brand Placement Representative, Certification, CRM, Evidence, Independent representation, Inference, Marketplace, Match thesis, Opportunity, Opportunity Network, Placement, Product, Professional Development, Relationship stewardship, Ryva Pro, Score, Three-party test, and Unknown.

The Frameworks use those definitions without changing them.

## Identified conflicts and ambiguities

1. The Standard's practice lifecycle is `define mandate → research → evaluate → qualify → match → prepare → engage → facilitate → document → steward → review`. The proposed Placement Cycle uses 12 market-facing stages. The new cycle must map to, not replace, the governing lifecycle.
2. The Standard defines **Opportunity**. The proposed term **Placement Opportunity** may be used only as a contextual subtype, not a competing definition.
3. **Brand Placement Closer**, **Protected Account**, **Placement Value**, **Representation Opportunity**, **Buyer Intelligence**, **Commission Intelligence**, and **Closing Program** are not established Standard terms.
4. “Place” must remain consistent with the Standard's definition of Placement and must not imply guaranteed sell-through or an ongoing result.
5. Baseline score weights, score bands, risk gates, and confidence thresholds are unresolved in the Standard.
6. Existing workspace materials outside `the-ryva-standard/` describe a conflicting AI-worker company identity. They are outside the authority for these Frameworks.

Unresolved matters are carried to the [Founder Decision Log](../05-governance/founder-decision-log.md).

## Framework design contract

Each framework must define:

- purpose and operating problem;
- appropriate and inappropriate use;
- inputs and evidence treatment;
- a repeatable method;
- a defined output and action rule;
- guardrails and human decision ownership;
- implementation across Certification, Ryva Pro, the proposed Closing Program, and Marketing; and
- unresolved founder decisions where a binding threshold, weight, or policy is not established.
