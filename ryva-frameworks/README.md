# The Ryva Frameworks

**Version:** 0.1  
**Status:** Complete first draft; not formally adopted  
**Governing authority:** [The Ryva Standard Version 1.0](../the-ryva-standard/README.md)  
**Document owner:** TODO (Founder Decision Required)  
**Effective date:** Not effective until approved

The Ryva Frameworks define the repeatable systems used to execute Brand Placement. They translate The Ryva Standard into decision models, scorecards, workflows, evidence rules, and implementation requirements for Certification, Ryva Pro, the proposed Closing Program, and Marketing.

The Frameworks are lower-order documentation. If a Framework conflicts with The Ryva Standard, the Standard governs and the conflict must be recorded. A Framework does not create legal authorization, regulatory clearance, guaranteed placement, guaranteed revenue, or guaranteed commission.

## Required interpretation

- Scores support named decisions; they do not declare objective worth.
- Five-point ratings use the anchors established in The Ryva Standard.
- “Unknown” is recorded as missing evidence, never as a midpoint.
- Confidence describes support for a conclusion, not the probability of commercial success.
- Gates and stop conditions take priority over totals.
- A high score cannot cure missing authority, misleading claims, material safety risk, fraud, or an ethics conflict.
- The Representative remains accountable for material recommendations and relationship decisions.
