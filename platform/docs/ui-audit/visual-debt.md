# Visual Debt

This document is intentionally direct. The application is functionally broad and ethically careful, but much of the interface still reads as a production-capable internal tool rather than a deliberately composed professional product.

## Findings

| ID | Severity | Debt | Why the product feels unfinished |
|---|---|---|---|
| VD-01 | Critical | Most operational pages are generic panel-and-form CRUD compositions. | Domain meaning is conveyed by copy, while the visual structure changes little between evidence, authority, outreach, orders, and settings. |
| VD-02 | High | Panels are overused as the default container. | Registers, forms, warnings, metrics, and histories receive similar boxes, borders, radii, padding, and shadows, flattening priority. |
| VD-03 | High | List pages frequently place a creation form beside or below the register. | Screens look like administrative back-office tools and make browsing versus creating feel unresolved. |
| VD-04 | High | Long detail pages repeat summary cards, definition grids, forms, lists, and timelines without a strong decision spine. | Users see completeness but not a clear visual answer to “what matters now?” |
| VD-05 | High | Navigation is text-heavy, flat, and crowded. | The product’s breadth is exposed as a directory rather than an intentional work environment. |
| VD-06 | Medium | Empty datasets create large, repetitive blank panels. | The application can feel skeletal even though the empty-state copy is responsible and specific. |
| VD-07 | Medium | Analytics resembles a generic dashboard despite avoiding fabricated metrics. | Repeated metric cards and tables lack a distinctive visual relationship to decisions, evidence, or pipeline flow. |
| VD-08 | Medium | Data presentation changes arbitrarily between cards, lists, tables, detail grids, and metrics. | Similar records do not build a stable scan pattern or density expectation. |
| VD-09 | Medium | The absence of an icon/illustration/visual cue system makes every action and destination typographically similar. | The interface is clear but visually monotonous, especially in dense navigation and action rows. |
| VD-10 | Medium | Loading, empty, warning, blocked, unavailable, and error states do not form one polished state language. | Some states feel like final product surfaces; others feel like inline implementation messages. |

## Brutally honest assessment by theme

### Generic CRUD feeling

The dominant page grammar is: large title, explanatory paragraph, one or more white panels, a two-column form, a list/table, and status pills. The words change; the composition often does not. This undersells the sophistication of evidence provenance, human authority, relationship protection, and commercial continuity.

### Excessive cards and borders

Panel shadows and borders surround nearly every unit. Within panels, list rows and detail grids add more borders. The nested containment creates visual clutter while still failing to establish which boundary represents a record, a decision, a warning, or merely layout.

### Weak hierarchy

The product voice provides excellent ethical context, but long descriptions, large headings, metric cards, and repeated section headings all compete for attention. Consequential blockers can be visually similar to provider notices or standard panels. Primary next action is not consistently anchored.

### Poor density calibration

The design alternates between oversized headings/panel padding and very small table/status metadata. Operational users get neither a consistently spacious review mode nor a consistently efficient work mode. Empty pages feel too spacious; populated forms and tables feel dense.

### Repetitive layouts

Brands, Products, Buyers, Representation, Placements, Outreach, Commerce, and utilities repeat split grids and panels. This creates consistency at a low level but not meaningful differentiation by workflow.

### Placeholder feeling without fake functionality

There is no fake live data and the features are real. The placeholder feeling comes instead from broad empty registers, generic labels, missing visual orientation, raw enum statuses, and local patterns that have not yet matured into a coherent system.

### Dashboard appearance

Home and Analytics responsibly avoid misleading scores, yet their metric-card-and-table format looks conventional. The distinctive Ryva concepts—evidence class, human judgment, authority, relationship triangle, and next responsible action—are mostly text rather than the organizing visual structure.
