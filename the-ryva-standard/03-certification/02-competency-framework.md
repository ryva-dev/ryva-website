# Competency Framework

## 1. Structure

Competence combines knowledge, applied skill, professional judgment, and observable conduct. The framework defines the minimum domains for initial certification; it does not prescribe a course sequence.

Proficiency levels are:

- **Awareness:** recognizes the concept and its relevance.
- **Working:** performs routine work accurately using established standards.
- **Applied judgment:** adapts the standard to unfamiliar or ambiguous situations and explains the decision.

Initial certification requires at least Working proficiency in every domain and Applied judgment where specified below.

## 2. Domains

### Domain 1: Profession and role

The candidate can:

- define Brand Placement and distinguish it from manufacturing, inventory ownership, retail operation, and indiscriminate lead generation;
- explain the Brand–Business–Representative relationship;
- distinguish certification from licensing; and
- identify the limits of a Representative's authority.

**Minimum:** Applied judgment.

### Domain 2: Ethics and professional conduct

The candidate can:

- apply the Code of Ethics;
- recognize conflicts, misleading claims, improper incentives, and confidentiality risks;
- disclose limitations appropriately; and
- choose a defensible action in an ambiguous scenario.

**Minimum:** Applied judgment.

### Domain 3: Product and Brand evaluation

The candidate can:

- gather and validate relevant evidence;
- assess review quality, sales evidence, repeat-purchase potential, retail readiness, margin quality, Business fit, and trend strength;
- distinguish Product quality from Brand readiness;
- explain uncertainty and missing evidence; and
- avoid excluding a Brand merely because it is small.

**Minimum:** Applied judgment.

### Domain 4: Business and buyer qualification

The candidate can:

- define an ideal Business profile for a Product;
- evaluate customer, channel, positioning, economic, operational, and timing fit;
- identify a legitimate buyer and decision path; and
- apply fair and relevant qualification criteria.

**Minimum:** Working.

### Domain 5: Opportunity analysis and matching

The candidate can:

- form a match thesis;
- compare potential placements;
- identify stakeholder value and risk;
- distinguish an introduction from a qualified Opportunity; and
- state the evidence that would advance or disqualify the Opportunity.

**Minimum:** Applied judgment.

### Domain 6: Commercial fundamentals

The candidate can:

- interpret common pricing, margin, commission, territory, exclusivity, order, fulfillment, return, and payment concepts;
- identify when a term requires clarification or specialist advice;
- understand the relationship between Product economics and Business viability; and
- avoid promising terms outside their authority.

**Minimum:** Working.

This domain teaches commercial literacy, not legal, tax, or financial advice.

### Domain 7: Professional communication and relationship development

The candidate can:

- prepare relevant outreach;
- communicate a clear, accurate value rationale;
- ask and respond to qualification questions;
- handle objections without pressure or misrepresentation;
- document next steps; and
- maintain a relationship after the initial transaction.

**Minimum:** Applied judgment.

### Domain 8: Workflow and record management

The candidate can:

- maintain accurate Brand, Product, Business, contact, Opportunity, activity, and outcome records;
- protect confidential and personal information;
- use reminders and stages without mistaking activity for progress; and
- preserve evidence for decisions and follow-through.

**Minimum:** Working.

### Domain 9: Data, analytics, and scoring

The candidate can:

- interpret scores, weights, confidence, freshness, and evidence quality;
- explain why a score is not a guarantee;
- identify missing or biased data;
- compare like with like; and
- override a recommendation only with a documented professional rationale.

**Minimum:** Applied judgment.

### Domain 10: Responsible use of AI

The candidate can:

- use AI for research assistance, preparation, organization, and analysis;
- verify material outputs;
- protect confidential information;
- recognize fabricated or weakly supported content;
- maintain responsibility for decisions and communications; and
- avoid deceptive automation or false personalization.

**Minimum:** Applied judgment.

### Domain 11: Relationship operations

The candidate can:

- coordinate handoffs and commitments;
- surface delays and issues early;
- support accurate communication among parties;
- review placement and repeat-purchase evidence; and
- close, pause, or revise an Opportunity professionally.

**Minimum:** Working.

### Domain 12: Ownership and professional development

The candidate can:

- plan and prioritize work;
- manage commitments reliably;
- identify a personal competence gap;
- use feedback and outcome evidence to improve; and
- comply with continuing-education and renewal requirements.

**Minimum:** Working.

## 3. Assessment mapping

Every mandatory competency must map to at least one scored assessment element. High-consequence Applied judgment competencies should not be assessed solely by recall questions.

The assessment blueprint must specify domain coverage, cognitive level, evidence type, scoring weight, and critical-fail conditions.
