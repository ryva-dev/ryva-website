# Certification Standard

## 1. Purpose and claim

Ryva certification validates that an individual has demonstrated the defined entry-level competencies required to practice Brand Placement under the Ryva Standard.

Certification is:

- a private professional certification;
- based on demonstrated competency;
- time-limited; and
- subject to continuing compliance and renewal.

Certification is not:

- government licensing;
- a guarantee of work, income, sales, or Product performance;
- evidence of competence outside the assessed scope; or
- permanent.

## 2. Independence of the credential

The credibility of certification must take priority over enrollment, completion, subscription, and marketplace-growth targets. Commercial teams must not control assessment outcomes for individual candidates.

Training and assessment may exist within the same organization only with controls that protect assessment validity, consistent standards, and conflict management.

## 3. Eligibility

Eligibility requirements must be relevant, lawful, accessible, and publicly understandable. They must not create artificial barriers unrelated to professional competence.

## 4. Certification requirements

To earn certification, a candidate must:

1. satisfy approved eligibility and identity requirements;
2. demonstrate every mandatory competency domain;
3. pass the approved assessment requirements;
4. accept the Code of Ethics and certification terms; and
5. have no unresolved condition that makes the credential misleading.

Course attendance alone must not establish competence.

## 5. Assessment

Assessment must be aligned to the [competency framework](02-competency-framework.md) and use sufficient evidence to support the certification claim. It should combine knowledge assessment with applied judgment and work-product evidence where appropriate.

Before release, each assessment form must have documented:

- blueprint alignment;
- scoring rules and pass logic;
- security and identity controls;
- accessibility treatment;
- reviewer or automated-scoring quality controls;
- retake and appeal rules; and
- evidence that the form is fit for its intended decision.

## 6. Term and renewal

Certification expires two years after the effective certification or renewal date.

Renewal must focus on:

- changes to the Ryva Standard;
- updated professional, legal, ethical, and technology requirements relevant to practice;
- continuing education; and
- continued good standing.

Renewal must not require repeating the full initial certification unless the credential has lapsed beyond an approved period, evidence indicates competence must be re-established, or the certification scope has materially changed.

## 7. Good standing

Good standing requires:

- a current, unexpired credential;
- compliance with the Code of Ethics and certification terms;
- completion of required continuing education;
- accurate use of the designation; and
- no active suspension or revocation.

## 8. Certification lifecycle

Ryva must maintain controlled processes for application, eligibility, assessment, award, public verification, renewal, lapse, suspension, revocation, surrender, reinstatement, and appeal.

Status changes must be recorded with effective dates. Public status must reveal no more personal information than necessary.

## 9. Access to Ryva Pro

Only certified Representatives may gain access to Ryva Pro. Access also depends on account status, acceptance of applicable terms, security requirements, and compliance with product policies. Certification alone does not override a suspension, legal restriction, or security control.

## 10. Advanced sales program

Ryva may offer an optional advanced sales program for additional coaching and sales mastery. It must remain distinct from the minimum certification unless the certification scope is formally amended. Its marketing must not imply that purchasing it is required for fair assessment or access to ordinary certification.
