# Curriculum Philosophy and Assessment Standard

## 1. Curriculum purpose

Ryva curriculum prepares candidates and certified Representatives to meet defined professional competencies. It must not be designed as content volume, entertainment, motivation, or a proxy for assessment.

## 2. Design sequence

Curriculum design follows:

`professional outcome → competency → observable performance → assessment evidence → learning experience → supporting content`

Content without a mapped competency or required context should not be included.

## 3. Curriculum principles

Curriculum must:

- reflect the current Standard and distinguish requirements from examples;
- teach principles before tools and tactics;
- use realistic Brand Placement decisions and work products;
- address ethical and commercial tradeoffs;
- teach evidence quality, uncertainty, and limits;
- prepare candidates to work without dependence on a script;
- be accessible and understandable to the intended audience;
- state when legal, regulatory, tax, privacy, or category-specific expertise is required; and
- be versioned so a learner's instruction can be traced to the applicable standard.

Examples, platform demonstrations, and channel tactics should be replaceable without destabilizing the underlying competency model.

## 4. Learning modes

Ryva may use self-directed instruction, instructor-led sessions, practice environments, coaching, case analysis, simulations, peer review, and supervised work. The mode must fit the competency rather than commercial packaging preferences.

## 5. Assessment philosophy

Assessment determines whether evidence supports a competency decision. It must be:

- valid for the stated competency;
- reliable enough for the consequence of the decision;
- fair and accessible;
- secure and identity-aware;
- scored against defined criteria;
- independent of candidate popularity or commercial value; and
- reviewable through a controlled appeal process.

Feedback for learning and decisions for certification are related but distinct. Coaching must not reveal secure assessment content or guarantee an outcome.

## 6. Instructors, coaches, and assessors

People who teach or assess must be qualified for their assigned role, trained on the current Standard, and subject to quality review. An assessor must disclose conflicts and must not alter a result to satisfy a sales, completion, or relationship target.

## 7. Continuing education

Continuing education should address changes in standards, material developments in practice, demonstrated competence gaps, and advanced practice. Completion evidence must be proportionate to the learning claim.

Promotional content, ordinary product onboarding, and activity without a learning objective must not automatically qualify.

## 8. Advanced sales development

The optional advanced sales program may develop deeper communication, negotiation, account strategy, and relationship mastery. It must remain consistent with the Code of Ethics and must not teach pressure, concealment, artificial scarcity, or commission-first behavior.

## 9. Curriculum governance

Each module must have an owner, competency mapping, version, review date, source record, and retirement rule. Material changes to the Standard require an impact review across curriculum, assessment, instructor guidance, and learner communications.
