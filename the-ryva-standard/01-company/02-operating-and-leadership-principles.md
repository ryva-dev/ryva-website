# Operating and Leadership Principles

## 1. Purpose before activity

Ryva measures work by the professional or commercial outcome it advances, not by the volume of content, features, contacts, lessons, or transactions produced.

## 2. The three-party test

Decisions affecting a Brand Placement relationship must consider the Brand, the Business, and the Representative. A model that depends on one party being misled, underinformed, or persistently disadvantaged is not acceptable.

Equal benefit is not required in every transaction. Informed participation, credible value, and sustainable economics are.

## 3. Trust before commission

Revenue does not cure a breach of trust. Ryva and its Representatives must not sacrifice accuracy, fit, disclosure, or long-term relationships to accelerate a commission or platform outcome.

## 4. Standards before scale

Ryva must define the competence, evidence, controls, and support required for a model before expanding it. Scale that makes conduct less inspectable or certification less credible is not progress.

## 5. Evidence before certainty

Recommendations must be proportionate to the quality, relevance, and freshness of evidence. Teams must distinguish fact, inference, estimate, opinion, and unknown.

## 6. Professional judgment within boundaries

Standards should enable judgment, not replace it with rigid scripts. Judgment must remain inside ethical, legal, contractual, and evidentiary boundaries and must be explainable after the fact.

## 7. Durable systems over heroics

Repeated work should become a documented, teachable, and inspectable system. Ryva must not depend on individual memory or exceptional effort for routine quality.

## 8. Clear ownership

Every material initiative, metric, risk, and decision must have one accountable owner. Collaboration does not divide accountability.

## 9. Reliability is active

Owners must surface risk before a commitment fails. Silence, optimistic ambiguity, and late disclosure are not reliable behavior.

## 10. Correct the system

When an error occurs, Ryva must correct the affected outcome where possible and examine the conditions that permitted it. Accountability includes individual conduct and system design.

## 11. Technology serves the profession

Ryva adopts technology when it improves judgment, reduces administrative work, strengthens evidence, or protects relationships. Novelty alone is not a product reason.

## 12. Human relationships remain human

AI may help prepare, organize, analyze, and recommend. It must not create false intimacy, impersonate a Representative, conceal material automation, or remove human accountability from a commercial relationship.

## 13. Access follows competence

Access to higher-risk capabilities and network privileges should follow demonstrated competence, appropriate permissions, and continued good standing. Certification is the minimum gateway to Ryva Pro, not a universal grant of authority.

## 14. Leadership obligations

Ryva leaders must:

- set explicit standards and decision rights;
- make tradeoffs visible;
- allocate resources consistently with the four pillars;
- protect the credibility of certification;
- invite material contrary evidence;
- avoid incentives that reward overselling or poor fit;
- resolve or formally defer decisions rather than allowing ambiguity to become custom; and
- apply the Standard consistently to executive, commercial, product, and instructional work.

Leaders must not use urgency to bypass integrity, conceal uncertainty, or transfer unacknowledged risk to Representatives, Brands, or Businesses.
