# Company Foundation

## 1. Identity

Ryva is the professional home of Brand Placement. It modernizes and standardizes the existing profession of independent wholesale representation through certification, professional development, purpose-built software, and an opportunity network.

Ryva did not invent wholesale sales. It defines **Brand Placement** as a modern professional standard for the work of identifying high-potential Products, matching them with qualified Business Buyers, and building durable commercial relationships through independent representation.

Ryva is not a course company. Education is a means of establishing and maintaining professional competence; it is not the company's complete purpose.

## 2. Mission

Ryva exists to create growth for Brands entering new business relationships while allowing millions of people to participate in that growth through a flexible career that rewards effort instead of hours worked.

The reference to “millions” expresses intended scale. It must not be used as a claim about present participation, earnings, or outcomes.

## 3. Vision

Ryva will become the place every Brand Placement Representative starts.

The vision sets direction. It does not justify lowering certification requirements, overstating opportunity, or serving people for whom the profession is unsuitable.

## 4. The four pillars

Every material initiative must strengthen at least one pillar and must not materially weaken another.

### 4.1 Certification

Certification establishes a common entry standard and validates competency. It is the foundation of the Ryva system.

### 4.2 Professional Development

Professional Development maintains and extends competence after initial certification. It includes continuing education and may include optional advanced sales development.

### 4.3 Ryva Pro

Ryva Pro is the operating system for Brand Placement. It provides the workflow, intelligence, records, and analytical support Representatives need to make better decisions and devote more time to commercial relationships.

### 4.4 Opportunity Network

The Opportunity Network connects qualified participants to suitable commercial opportunities under transparent, professional rules. It must increase fit and reduce search friction without converting access into a promise of work or income.

## 5. Problems Ryva addresses

Ryva exists in response to:

- outdated practices in wholesale representation;
- fragmented education and inconsistent training;
- inefficient Product, Brand, and Business research;
- technology that does not reflect the Representative's work;
- inconsistent professional standards; and
- uses of AI that displace or degrade genuine human relationship-building.

These are operating problems, not permission to disparage the wider wholesale industry or people working within it.

## 6. Customer fit

Ryva is designed for people who seek schedule ownership, performance-based income, and responsibility for building long-term business relationships. Discipline, reliability, analytical judgment, professional conduct, and sustained effort are more relevant than age.

The founding customer description identifies a young, enthusiastic, disciplined, motivated, and driven audience. Ryva must not turn “young” into an exclusionary eligibility rule unless a lawful and justified minimum-age requirement applies.

Ryva is not positioned as:

- easy or passive income;
- a shortcut to commercial success;
- a get-rich-quick opportunity; or
- a substitute for competence and consistent work.

## 7. Core values

### Professionalism

Work is prepared, accurate, appropriate to context, and respectful of the parties affected.

### Integrity

Claims, records, recommendations, incentives, and limitations are represented truthfully.

### Long-Term Thinking

Ryva favors durable trust and sustainable economics over a short-term transaction.

### Ownership

Individuals accept responsibility for preparation, decisions, follow-through, correction, and outcomes within their control.

### Reliability

Commitments are explicit, realistic, tracked, and completed or renegotiated before failure.

Values are decision standards. They are not slogans and must be reflected in product requirements, assessment, performance management, and partner selection.
