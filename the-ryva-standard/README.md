# The Ryva Standard

**Version:** 1.0  
**Status:** Founding standard  
**Scope:** Ryva and the Brand Placement profession  
**Document owner:** TODO (Founder Decision Required)  
**Effective date:** TODO (Founder Decision Required)

The Ryva Standard is the governing documentation for Ryva. It defines the profession Ryva serves, the principles under which the company operates, the requirements of certification, and the boundaries of Ryva's products and network.

This repository is the source of truth for decisions within its scope. Product specifications, curricula, procedures, commercial plans, and public communications must conform to it. It is not a substitute for law, regulation, contractual obligations, or specialist advice.

Material version history is maintained in the [change log](CHANGELOG.md).

## Normative language

The words **must**, **must not**, **should**, **should not**, and **may** are deliberate.

- **Must / must not**: a binding requirement of this standard.
- **Should / should not**: the expected approach; an exception requires a documented reason.
- **May**: a permitted option, not a requirement.

Descriptive text explains intent. Where descriptive text and a stated requirement conflict, the stated requirement governs.

## Scope boundaries

The Standard governs:

- the definition and professional practice of Brand Placement;
- Ryva's company philosophy, operating principles, and four pillars;
- certification, competency, curriculum, and assessment;
- professional ethics and representative conduct;
- the intended role of Ryva Pro, data, analytics, AI, CRM, and intelligence;
- the Opportunity Network, marketplace, and professional community; and
- the way Ryva makes and records decisions.

The Standard does not establish:

- government licensing or legal authorization to practice;
- employment, agency, tax, antitrust, privacy, or financial advice;
- specific pricing, commission rates, examination scores, or commercial terms not yet approved;
- product functionality merely because a capability is described as a future direction; or
- a guarantee of income, placement, product performance, or commercial success.

## Version 1.0 interpretation

Version 1.0 distinguishes established doctrine from unresolved policy. Unresolved matters are labeled **TODO (Founder Decision Required)** and listed in the [founder decision register](07-reference/02-founder-decision-register.md). A TODO is not permission to improvise. Until resolved, teams must use the narrowest reversible approach consistent with established principles and obtain the appropriate decision before creating a binding policy or public promise.
