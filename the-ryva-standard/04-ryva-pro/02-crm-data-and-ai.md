# CRM, Data, and AI Philosophy

## 1. CRM philosophy

The CRM component of Ryva Pro is the professional memory of Brand Placement. It exists to preserve context, commitments, decisions, relationship history, and next actions.

It must model the profession rather than force it into a generic sales funnel. At minimum, the model should distinguish:

- Brands and their mandates;
- Products and evidence;
- Businesses and locations;
- people, roles, and contact authority;
- Brand–Representative and Business–Representative relationships;
- Opportunities and match rationales;
- communications, activities, commitments, and follow-ups;
- commercial terms and their status;
- orders or outcomes where available; and
- stewardship, repeat-purchase, and relationship health.

Pipeline stage is an operational summary, not proof of buyer intent or commercial value.

## 2. Data philosophy

Ryva collects and processes data to support a defined professional, certification, security, or operational purpose. It must not collect data merely because it may become useful.

Material data must have:

- a defined owner or steward;
- a stated source and observation time;
- a classification for fact, inference, estimate, or user assertion;
- an appropriate freshness expectation;
- access and use boundaries;
- a correction path; and
- a retention or deletion rule.

Ryva must distinguish data belonging to or provided by a Brand, Business, Representative, or Ryva. Access to data does not imply unrestricted ownership or reuse.

## 3. AI philosophy

AI exists to increase the Representative's analytical capacity and reduce administrative burden while preserving genuine human relationship-building.

Appropriate uses may include:

- organizing and summarizing authorized information;
- identifying missing evidence;
- comparing Products, Brands, Businesses, and Opportunities;
- drafting research plans and communication options;
- suggesting next actions;
- detecting stale records or unresolved commitments; and
- explaining factors that contribute to a score or recommendation.

AI must not:

- fabricate evidence, citations, demand, interest, or outcomes;
- make an undisclosed identity claim or impersonate a Representative;
- send material relationship communications without the approved level of human control;
- represent an inference as an observed fact;
- use confidential information outside its authorized purpose;
- make final certification, ethics, or disciplinary decisions without the approved accountable process; or
- optimize for contact volume at the expense of relevance, consent, or trust.

## 4. Human accountability

The user or designated Ryva owner remains accountable for material decisions. “The AI recommended it” is not a professional rationale.

For a material AI-supported recommendation, Ryva Pro should make it possible to inspect:

- the intended task;
- material input evidence;
- important assumptions and missing information;
- the recommendation and confidence or uncertainty;
- the decision made by the responsible person; and
- later correction or outcome.

## 5. Automation levels

Automation must be explicit:

1. **Assist:** generate options or analysis for human review.
2. **Prepare:** assemble a proposed record, task, or communication.
3. **Execute bounded internal work:** update approved internal state under defined rules.
4. **Execute external work:** act outside Ryva under explicit authority, review, and safeguards.

Certification does not itself authorize external automation. Users must understand the active level and be able to stop or correct it.

## 6. Evaluation

AI quality must be evaluated on professional usefulness, factual support, error severity, bias, privacy, explainability, and effect on relationship conduct. Output volume and user engagement are insufficient quality measures.
