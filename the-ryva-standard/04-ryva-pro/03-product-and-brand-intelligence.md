# Product and Brand Intelligence

## 1. Purpose

Product and Brand intelligence converts relevant evidence into an explainable view of commercial potential, readiness, fit, and risk. It supports prioritization and professional judgment; it does not guarantee placement or performance.

## 2. Separate objects

A Product and its Brand must be evaluated separately.

- **Product intelligence** concerns the offer itself, its evidence, customer demand, economics, readiness, differentiation, and fit.
- **Brand intelligence** concerns the organization’s ability and willingness to support a durable commercial relationship.

A strong Product may belong to a Brand that is not ready for expansion. A capable Brand may offer a Product that is not suitable for a specific Business.

## 3. Evidence hierarchy

Evidence should be weighted by relevance, directness, quality, and freshness. Typical classes include:

1. verified first-party commercial and operational records;
2. verified Business or channel outcomes;
3. credible independent market and customer evidence;
4. Brand-supplied claims and materials;
5. Representative observations;
6. model-derived inference or proxy evidence.

Lower-ranked evidence is not automatically invalid. Its limitations must be visible and it must not be presented as stronger evidence.

## 4. Product intelligence dimensions

Product evaluation should consider:

- review quality and review integrity;
- sales evidence and the comparability of the sales context;
- repeat-purchase potential;
- retail or Business readiness;
- margin quality and commercial viability;
- fit for the target Business and its customers;
- trend strength, durability, and saturation;
- differentiation and substitutability;
- fulfillment, support, return, and operational requirements;
- claims, compliance, safety, and reputational risk; and
- evidence completeness and freshness.

Not every dimension applies equally to every Product category. Any excluded dimension or changed weight requires a rationale.

## 5. Brand intelligence dimensions

Brand evaluation should consider:

- clarity of the representation mandate;
- Product and market evidence;
- commercial terms and Representative economics;
- inventory and fulfillment reliability;
- responsiveness and operational support;
- quality and accuracy of sales materials;
- channel strategy and conflict risk;
- returns, complaints, and issue handling;
- legal, compliance, safety, and reputation signals;
- data quality and willingness to share necessary information;
- relationship orientation and repeat-business support; and
- readiness for the target channel, territory, and pace of expansion.

Brand size, age, funding, or fame must not be an automatic acceptance or rejection rule.

## 6. Business fit

Fit is contextual. It connects a Product and Brand to a specific Business, channel, location, customer base, price architecture, margin requirement, assortment, operational capacity, timing, and relationship objective.

A general Product or Brand score must not be represented as a Business-fit score.

## 7. Unknown and adverse evidence

Missing evidence must remain visible. The absence of a negative signal is not positive evidence. Material adverse evidence must not be removed from a score or recommendation merely because it lowers commercial attractiveness.

## 8. Intelligence governance

Ryva must define source rights, update cadence, dispute and correction handling, model validation, and audit records before using intelligence for consequential access or ranking decisions.
