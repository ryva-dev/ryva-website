# Community Philosophy

## 1. Role

Community is a supporting capability, not Ryva's primary product. The initial priority is professional development. Community should expand when Ryva can maintain useful expertise, clear conduct standards, and responsible moderation.

## 2. Long-term functions

Ryva community may support:

- mentoring;
- collaboration;
- knowledge sharing; and
- professional guidance.

It must not become a substitute for verified intelligence, formal certification decisions, professional accountability, or confidential Brand and Business communication.

## 3. Knowledge quality

Community content must distinguish personal experience, opinion, observed evidence, and Ryva-approved guidance. Popularity does not establish accuracy.

Commercial affiliations, referral interests, and material incentives must be disclosed when they could affect a recommendation.

## 4. Conduct

Participants must:

- communicate professionally;
- protect confidential and personal information;
- avoid harassment, discrimination, manipulation, and deceptive promotion;
- respect intellectual property and contractual restrictions;
- avoid collusion or improper sharing of competitively sensitive information; and
- correct material inaccuracies they introduced.

## 5. Mentoring

Mentoring must have clear scope and expectations. A mentor's experience does not authorize them to override the Standard, guarantee outcomes, or act as legal, tax, or financial adviser without the relevant qualification.

## 6. Moderation

Moderation rules must be written, consistently applied, and subject to proportionate review. Ryva must distinguish disagreement from misconduct and preserve space for good-faith professional criticism.
