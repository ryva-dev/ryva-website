# Opportunity Framework and Marketplace Philosophy

## 1. Purpose

The Opportunity Network reduces the friction of finding suitable Brands, Representatives, Businesses, and commercial relationships. It serves the profession; it is not a guarantee of work, placement, access, response, order, commission, or income.

The marketplace is a capability within the Opportunity Network, not Ryva's complete identity.

## 2. Opportunity definition

An **Opportunity** is a documented potential Brand–Business relationship for which there is a credible match thesis, an identifiable next step, and sufficient authority to pursue evaluation.

A listing, lead, contact, Product, or introduction is not automatically an Opportunity.

## 3. Opportunity stages

The network should distinguish:

1. **Candidate:** a potential match requiring research.
2. **Qualified:** minimum Brand, Product, Business, and authority conditions are met.
3. **Engaged:** an authorized party has entered a relevant commercial conversation.
4. **Evaluating:** fit, terms, or operational requirements are under active review.
5. **Committed:** parties have recorded an agreed commercial next step.
6. **Active relationship:** the placement or commercial relationship is operating.
7. **Closed:** won, lost, withdrawn, disqualified, expired, or deferred with a recorded reason.

Stages describe evidence. They must not be advanced to make performance appear stronger.

## 4. Match framework

Opportunity quality should consider:

- Product–Business fit;
- Brand readiness and mandate;
- Representative competence, capacity, territory, conflicts, and relationship;
- Business qualification and timing;
- commercial viability for each party;
- evidence quality and freshness;
- relationship and reputational risk; and
- the clarity of the next professional action.

## 5. Access principles

Network access must be governed by certification, good standing, relevant permissions, and fit. Ryva may apply additional competence, category, geographic, capacity, or performance requirements where they are transparent and relevant.

Paying more must not allow a participant to bypass ethics, certification, qualification, or disclosure requirements.

## 6. Allocation and ranking

Ranking and allocation must be explainable enough for affected users to understand the principal factors. Ryva must manage conflicts created by sponsorship, fees, commissions, exclusivity, or its own commercial interests.

Sponsored or paid placement must be labeled and must not be represented as an objective fit ranking.

## 7. Marketplace conduct

Participants must provide accurate identity, authority, Product, commercial, and availability information. They must use network information only for the intended professional purpose and respect confidentiality, contact preferences, and applicable agreements.

Ryva must provide mechanisms to report inaccurate listings, misconduct, conflicts, and unsuitable matches.

## 8. Opportunity quality measures

Ryva should evaluate the network using qualified-match rate, accepted introduction rate, progression quality, time to a meaningful decision, sustainable commercial relationships, repeat activity, dispute rate, and stakeholder experience.

Listing volume, contact volume, and gross introductions are not sufficient measures.
