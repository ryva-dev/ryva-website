# Authority and Governance

## 1. Purpose

The Ryva Standard converts the founding intent of Ryva into a durable system of definitions, requirements, and decision rules. Its purpose is to prevent local decisions from weakening the profession, the certification, or the trust among Brands, Businesses, Representatives, and Ryva.

## 2. Authority

Within Ryva, this Standard governs all lower-order documentation in its scope. The order of authority is:

1. applicable law and regulation;
2. binding contractual and regulatory obligations;
3. The Ryva Standard;
4. formally approved policies and product standards;
5. operating procedures, playbooks, curricula, and specifications;
6. team conventions and individual judgment.

Lower-order material must not contradict higher-order authority. When a conflict is found, the owner of the lower-order material must stop relying on the conflicting provision, document the conflict, and seek correction.

## 3. Document classes

Ryva maintains four document classes:

| Class | Function | Approval requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Standard | Defines enduring principles, boundaries, or mandatory requirements | TODO (Founder Decision Required): approving authority |
| Policy | Applies a standard to a defined subject or risk | Accountable executive and required specialist review |
| Procedure | Defines repeatable execution | Functional owner |
| Record | Preserves evidence of a decision, action, assessment, or result | Owner designated by the governing policy |

Marketing copy, sales material, help content, and training content are interpretations. They are not sources of governing doctrine.

## 4. Change control

Each change to the Standard must:

1. identify the requirement or ambiguity being addressed;
2. state the proposed change and affected documents;
3. assess effects on certification holders, candidates, Brands, Businesses, product behavior, and existing commitments;
4. identify required legal, privacy, security, accessibility, psychometric, or commercial review;
5. provide a migration or transition rule where expectations change;
6. receive the designated approval;
7. update cross-references and the decision register; and
8. be released with a version and effective date.

Editorial corrections that do not alter meaning may use a patch version. New compatible requirements should use a minor version. Changes that materially redefine the profession, certification promise, or stakeholder obligations require a major version.

## 5. Review standard

The Standard should receive a scheduled review at least annually and an event-driven review when:

- law or regulation affects the profession or Ryva;
- evidence shows a standard creates material harm or fails its purpose;
- the certification promise, product model, or Opportunity Network materially changes;
- a new stakeholder class or market is introduced; or
- repeated exceptions indicate that a requirement is unclear or impractical.

A review does not require change. The review record must state whether the provision remains suitable.

## 6. Exceptions

An exception may not silently become policy. A valid exception record states:

- the requirement affected;
- the reason strict compliance is not suitable;
- the duration and scope;
- affected parties and risks;
- compensating controls;
- approving authority; and
- the condition for expiry or review.

No exception may waive integrity, truthful representation, legal compliance, protection of confidential information, or the prohibition on misleading commercial claims.

## 7. Evidence and traceability

Material decisions must preserve the facts, assumptions, inferences, alternatives, owner, approval, date, and review condition. Data used to score a Product, Brand, Business, Representative, or Opportunity must be traceable to its source and observation date.

## 8. Public claims

Ryva must distinguish:

- current capability from planned capability;
- certification from government licensing;
- a score from a guarantee;
- observed fact from inference;
- professional guidance from legal or financial advice; and
- network access from an entitlement to opportunity or income.

No public claim may exceed the underlying evidence or governing standard.
