# Future Roadmap Doctrine

## 1. Long-term direction

Ryva is building:

- a professional standard;
- a private professional certification;
- professional development;
- purpose-built software;
- analytics and intelligence;
- responsible AI;
- an operating system for the profession; and
- an opportunity network.

The roadmap must reinforce Brand Placement rather than accumulate unrelated products.

## 2. Strategic sequence

The intended dependency order is:

1. **Define the profession.** Establish language, ethics, competencies, and relationship standards.
2. **Validate competence.** Build credible certification and continuing development.
3. **Support practice.** Deliver Ryva Pro workflows, records, and decision support.
4. **Improve intelligence.** Add evidence-based Product, Brand, Business, and Opportunity analysis.
5. **Connect opportunity.** Develop network and marketplace capabilities on a qualified base.
6. **Develop the profession.** Expand mentoring, collaboration, specializations, and advanced practice.

Work may overlap, but a later layer must not outrun the standards and controls on which it depends.

## 3. Roadmap admission criteria

An initiative should enter the committed roadmap only when it has:

- a defined user and professional problem;
- a clear relationship to at least one pillar;
- no unresolved contradiction with another pillar;
- an accountable owner;
- success and failure measures;
- material dependencies and risks;
- a decision on what remains human;
- required legal, privacy, security, certification, and ethical review; and
- an explicit statement of what will not be built.

## 4. Prioritization

Priority should reflect:

1. protection of trust, certification credibility, and existing commitments;
2. removal of barriers to competent professional practice;
3. improvement in relationship quality and sustainable commercial outcomes;
4. strength of evidence that Ryva can cause the intended improvement;
5. reuse across the profession and four pillars;
6. reversibility and learning value; and
7. cost and operational burden.

Revenue potential is relevant but does not override professional integrity or foundational dependencies.

## 5. Stage gates

### Explore

The problem, user, evidence, and alternatives are understood.

### Define

Requirements, boundaries, risks, metrics, ownership, and affected standards are documented.

### Validate

The proposed mechanism is tested with representative users and failure cases.

### Release

The capability is supportable, observable, secure, documented, and truthfully represented.

### Scale

Evidence shows useful outcomes without unacceptable conduct, quality, or operational effects.

### Retire

Data, users, commitments, and dependencies have a controlled transition.
