# Brand Placement Practice Standard

## 1. Practice lifecycle

Brand Placement is a managed lifecycle:

`define mandate → research → evaluate → qualify → match → prepare → engage → facilitate → document → steward → review`

The lifecycle may be iterative. A Representative must not skip a stage when doing so would create a material risk of poor fit, misrepresentation, or unmanaged obligations.

## 2. Define mandate

Before representing a Brand, the Representative must understand and document:

- the Products and approved claims;
- target Businesses, channels, and territories;
- pricing and commercial terms provided for representation;
- ordering, fulfillment, returns, and support responsibilities;
- compensation basis and timing;
- authority to communicate, negotiate, or bind;
- conflicts, restrictions, and exclusivity;
- use of trademarks, samples, and materials; and
- escalation contacts.

The Representative must not imply authority beyond the mandate.

## 3. Research and evaluate

Research must be sufficient to form a reasonable professional view of Product potential, Brand readiness, buyer relevance, and risk. Representatives must distinguish evidence supplied by a Brand from independently observed evidence.

Evaluation should include the criteria in the [scoring standard](../04-ryva-pro/04-scoring-standard.md). A score supports judgment; it does not replace verification or explainability.

## 4. Qualify the Business

A qualified Business has:

- a credible fit with the Product and intended customer;
- a legitimate business purpose;
- an identifiable decision process or authorized buyer;
- commercial and operational capacity appropriate to the opportunity; and
- no known disqualifying conduct or conflict.

Qualification must be proportionate. It must not rely on protected characteristics or irrelevant personal information.

## 5. Match

A match requires a documented rationale connecting Product characteristics and Brand requirements to the Business's customers, positioning, economics, channel, operating capacity, and timing.

Popularity alone is not fit. A large Business is not automatically better than a small one. A small Brand is not automatically less suitable than an established one.

## 6. Prepare and engage

Representatives must:

- use accurate, current, Brand-authorized materials;
- personalize the commercial rationale;
- state their role and represented interest;
- respect lawful contact preferences and platform rules;
- avoid artificial urgency, misleading scarcity, and unsupported claims; and
- preserve a professional record of material communications.

## 7. Facilitate

The Representative should help the parties clarify fit, terms, operational requirements, and next steps. The Representative must not conceal material limitations or create the impression that an unresolved term is agreed.

Legal, regulatory, product-safety, tax, or specialist questions must be referred to the responsible party or qualified adviser.

## 8. Document

Material facts, approvals, commitments, objections, changes, and outcomes must be recorded promptly. Records must be factual, respectful, access-controlled, and retained according to approved policy.

## 9. Steward

After an introduction or order, the Representative remains responsible for agreed relationship support. Stewardship includes monitoring commitments, surfacing issues early, supporting accurate communication, and identifying whether the relationship continues to benefit all parties.

Stewardship does not make the Representative responsible for functions assigned to the Brand or Business.

## 10. Review

The Representative should review:

- whether the placement matched the stated rationale;
- commercial and relationship outcomes;
- repeat-purchase evidence;
- complaints, returns, or operational failures;
- changes in Brand or Business fit; and
- lessons that should change future evaluation.

The review must not reinterpret weak results as success merely to preserve a score or commission.

## 11. Conflicts and disclosures

Representatives must disclose material conflicts, compensation structures when required or relevant to informed reliance, overlapping representation, and any personal or financial interest that could reasonably affect judgment.
