# Code of Ethics

## 1. Purpose

The Code of Ethics defines the minimum conduct required to preserve trust in Brand Placement. It applies to certified Representatives and should inform the conduct of Ryva employees, instructors, assessors, partners, and network participants.

## 2. Duties

### 2.1 Duty of honesty

State material facts accurately. Do not create, omit, or frame information in a way intended to mislead.

### 2.2 Duty of professional care

Use reasonable preparation, evidence, and judgment for the significance of the recommendation or action.

### 2.3 Duty to the relationship

Seek sustainable value for Brand, Business, and Representative. Do not treat a completed transaction as the sole measure of success.

### 2.4 Duty of independence

Exercise judgment within the agreed mandate. Do not allow commission, gifts, pressure, personal interest, or platform incentives to replace suitability.

### 2.5 Duty of disclosure

Disclose material interests, limitations, conflicts, and the capacity in which the individual is acting when they could affect informed judgment.

### 2.6 Duty of confidentiality

Use confidential and personal information only for legitimate authorized purposes. Limit access, sharing, and retention.

### 2.7 Duty of fairness

Apply relevant commercial criteria consistently. Do not exploit an information imbalance or use unlawful discrimination.

### 2.8 Duty of competence

Accept only work that can be performed competently or with suitable support. Maintain knowledge and acknowledge uncertainty.

### 2.9 Duty of accountability

Keep sufficient records, correct errors, cooperate with legitimate review, and accept consequences for professional conduct.

### 2.10 Duty to the profession

Do not misuse certification, manipulate professional standards, or engage in conduct that foreseeably undermines trust in Brand Placement.

## 3. Ethical decision rule

When an action is not expressly covered, the Representative must ask:

1. Is it truthful?
2. Is it within my authority and competence?
3. Would each affected party understand my role and incentives?
4. Does it preserve meaningful choice?
5. Can I explain the evidence and reasoning?
6. Would the action remain acceptable if documented and reviewed?
7. Does it support a sustainable relationship?

A negative or uncertain answer requires the Representative to stop, obtain guidance, disclose the issue, or choose a safer course.

## 4. Reporting concerns

Ryva must provide a confidential means to report suspected misconduct without retaliation for good-faith reporting. Reports must be handled consistently, with due process and protection against malicious allegations.
