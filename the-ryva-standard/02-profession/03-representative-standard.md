# Representative Standards

## 1. Professional duty

A Ryva Representative must create value for the Brand and Business while protecting the trust on which independent representation depends.

## 2. Required conduct

A Representative must:

- recommend Products they genuinely believe are suitable based on reasonable evaluation;
- prioritize long-term commercial relationships;
- prepare before making a recommendation or contact;
- think analytically and explain the basis of a match;
- represent Products, evidence, terms, and authority accurately;
- communicate material limitations and conflicts;
- maintain current, complete professional records;
- follow through on commitments;
- protect confidential and personal information;
- correct material errors promptly;
- remain within demonstrated competence and seek help when needed; and
- comply with applicable law, agreements, and Ryva requirements.

## 3. Prohibited conduct

A Representative must not:

- oversell a Product or make an unsupported performance claim;
- sacrifice trust for commission;
- fabricate demand, buyer interest, scarcity, reviews, relationships, or credentials;
- conceal compensation or conflicts when disclosure is necessary;
- misrepresent certification as licensing;
- imply that Ryva guarantees them, a Brand, a Business, or an outcome;
- use confidential information for an unrelated purpose;
- manipulate scores or omit adverse evidence;
- continue outreach after an applicable and valid opt-out;
- discriminate unlawfully or use irrelevant protected characteristics in qualification;
- accept a mandate they cannot service reliably; or
- use AI to impersonate a person or create deceptive relationship signals.

## 4. Competence and continuing development

Representatives are responsible for maintaining competence during the certification period. They must understand changes to standards relevant to their work and complete required continuing education.

Certification does not make every Representative competent for every category, channel, jurisdiction, or commercial arrangement. Representatives must identify the limits of their own experience.

## 5. Communications standard

Professional communications must be:

- accurate about identity, role, and purpose;
- relevant to the recipient;
- concise enough to support informed review;
- free of pressure that distorts consent;
- recorded when commercially material; and
- conducted through lawful, appropriate channels.

Templates may provide structure. They must not remove personalization or professional judgment.

## 6. Reliability standard

A Representative who cannot meet a commitment must notify affected parties before the commitment fails when reasonably possible, state the effect, provide a revised plan, and record the change.

## 7. Use of the Ryva designation

Only individuals in current good standing may represent themselves as Ryva-certified. They must use the exact designation approved by Ryva and must not imply employment, agency, endorsement, or authority that has not been granted.
