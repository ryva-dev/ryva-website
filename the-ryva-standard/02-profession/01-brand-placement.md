# Brand Placement

## 1. Normative definition

**Brand Placement is the professional practice of identifying high-potential Products, matching them with qualified Business Buyers, and creating long-term commercial relationships through independent representation.**

## 2. Professional objective

The objective is not merely to close an order. The objective is to establish a commercially sound relationship that can create durable value for:

- the Brand, through suitable new business relationships;
- the Business, through Products that fit its customers and commercial model; and
- the Representative, through fair compensation for creating and supporting the relationship.

A relationship is not sustainable when its economics or conduct systematically depend on one party not benefiting.

## 3. Role boundaries

In the ordinary Brand Placement model, Representatives:

- do not manufacture the Products represented;
- do not own the inventory;
- do not operate the purchasing store or Business; and
- do not become the Brand or Business merely by representing one party.

Representatives create value through discovery, evaluation, matching, introduction, commercial coordination, and relationship stewardship.

Where a person also manufactures, buys inventory, operates a store, or acts in another capacity, that role must be disclosed and separated. The existence of another role may create conflicts and may change legal or tax obligations.

## 4. Parties

### Brand

The entity offering a Product and seeking suitable business distribution or commercial relationships.

### Business

The organization evaluating or purchasing a Product for resale, use, service delivery, hospitality, corporate supply, or another legitimate business purpose.

### Business Buyer

The authorized person acting for a Business in evaluating or making a purchasing decision.

### Brand Placement Representative

An independent professional who identifies, evaluates, develops, and supports relationships between Brands and qualified Businesses.

### Ryva

The professional-standard, certification, product, development, and network organization supporting the profession.

## 5. What Brand Placement is not

Brand Placement is not:

- indiscriminate lead generation;
- an inventory resale model;
- a guarantee of sell-through or revenue;
- permission to misstate authority or exclusivity;
- a commission-first sales method;
- passive income;
- merely sending catalogs or introductions; or
- a substitute for the Brand's and Business's own commercial judgment.

## 6. Independent representation

“Independent” describes a commercial mode, not a universal legal classification. The actual relationship among a Representative, Brand, Business, and Ryva depends on the governing agreements and applicable law.

## 7. Professional legitimacy

Ryva's certification is a private professional certification. It validates competence against Ryva's requirements. It is not government licensing, does not confer a protected legal title unless law provides one, and does not guarantee commercial performance.
