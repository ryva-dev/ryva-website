# Pilot Instructions

## 1. Purpose

This procedure tests framework usability, not reviewer sales ability and not numerical performance. Reviewers should identify where the Frameworks help, where they fail, and what Ryva Pro must make visible.

## 2. Participants

The pilot may be completed by the Founder, an employee, an instructor candidate, or a future reviewer. For agreement testing, assign the same case to two reviewers working independently.

The Founder owns the Frameworks and approves revisions. Specialist review is requested only where the case genuinely requires legal, financial, regulatory, safety, or category expertise.

## 3. Timebox

Target 20–30 minutes per case:

| Activity | Target |
|---|---:|
| Read case and identify decision | 3 minutes |
| Classify material evidence and unknowns | 7 minutes |
| Allocate AI and human responsibility | 3 minutes |
| Identify Placement Cycle stage and next action | 4 minutes |
| Apply Relationship Triangle | 5 minutes |
| Apply Decision Filter and record outcome | 5 minutes |
| Record confusion and revisions | 3 minutes |

The timebox is diagnostic. If a case cannot be responsibly reviewed within 30 minutes, record why rather than rushing.

## 4. Procedure

### Step 1: Select a case

Record Case ID, reviewer, date, Framework version, and whether the review is independent or collaborative.

### Step 2: Review available evidence

Read only the case brief and linked internal definitions. Do not consult expected-answer guidance until the review is complete.

### Step 3: Classify evidence

Classify every material input as:

- Verified Fact;
- Direct Evidence;
- Strong Proxy;
- Weak Proxy;
- Estimate;
- Assumption;
- Model-Generated Inference; or
- Unknown.

State the exact claim each item can and cannot support.

### Step 4: Identify unknowns

Separate:

- decision-controlling unknowns;
- useful but non-controlling unknowns; and
- information that should not be collected because it is irrelevant, intrusive, or disproportionate.

### Step 5: Apply the Human Judgment Standard

Record:

- what AI may assist with;
- what requires human review;
- what cannot be delegated;
- which decision belongs to the Representative; and
- whether specialist review is required.

### Step 6: Identify the Placement Cycle stage

Record:

- current stage;
- completed entry criteria;
- missing exit criteria;
- next required action; and
- prohibited premature action.

Do not advance the case to make the pipeline appear stronger.

### Step 7: Review the Relationship Triangle

For Brand, Business Buyer, and Representative, record:

- value;
- obligations;
- risks;
- warning signs; and
- Supported, Conditional, or Unsupported condition.

The overall Triangle condition is controlled by the weakest side.

### Step 8: Apply the Decision Filter

Evaluate all ten questions as Pass, Conditional, Fail, or Unknown. Do not average the answers.

### Step 9: Record a decision

Select one:

- **Proceed**
- **Proceed With Conditions**
- **Investigate Further**
- **Do Not Proceed**

Record rationale, confidence, next action, owner, and review trigger.

### Step 10: Compare against expected guidance

For completed examples, compare with the worked review. For practice cases, use [Expected-Answer Guidance](cases/expected-answer-guidance.md). A different answer is not automatically wrong if it follows the Frameworks and identifies the evidence supporting the difference.

### Step 11: Record framework problems

Record:

- unclear evidence label;
- duplicated or contradictory rule;
- undefined term;
- missing stop condition;
- stage ambiguity;
- decision outcome ambiguity;
- unavailable or unreasonable data requirement;
- AI/human ownership uncertainty; or
- other usability issue.

### Step 12: Recommend revisions

Enter each proposed change in the [Framework Revision Log](framework-revision-log.md). Make the smallest change that solves the observed problem. Do not edit governing Frameworks during a review.

## 5. Decision interpretation

| Decision | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Proceed | Evidence supports the next bounded Placement Cycle action; no material condition prevents it |
| Proceed With Conditions | The next bounded action is permitted only under explicit conditions, limits, ownership, and review |
| Investigate Further | Material evidence is missing or contradictory; further diligence is the next action |
| Do Not Proceed | A material fail, stop condition, one-sided relationship, authority failure, or unmanageable risk prevents pursuit |

These outcomes apply to the current decision and stage. “Proceed” does not mean represent, approach, place, or guarantee success unless that is the exact bounded decision.

## 6. Agreement test

For paired reviews, compare:

- evidence class for each material input;
- decision-controlling unknowns;
- AI and human allocation;
- Placement Cycle stage;
- Triangle condition by party;
- Decision Filter result;
- final decision;
- next action; and
- confidence.

Record whether disagreement is caused by:

- different reading of case facts;
- ambiguous Framework wording;
- missing rule;
- different risk tolerance;
- category expertise;
- unsupported assumption; or
- clerical error.

Do not force agreement before recording the cause.

## 7. Pilot acceptance questions

At the end of the pilot, answer:

1. Can two reviewers reach similar conclusions?
2. Are evidence labels understandable?
3. Are missing facts visible?
4. Does the Placement Cycle clarify next action?
5. Does the Relationship Triangle prevent commission-first decisions?
6. Does the Human Judgment Standard preserve meaningful human responsibility?
7. Does the Decision Filter produce an actionable outcome?
8. Which terms are confusing?
9. Which rules duplicate or contradict?
10. Does the process require data Ryva Pro cannot reasonably collect?
11. Which fields must exist in the first platform version?
