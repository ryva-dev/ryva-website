# Completed Review — FP-003 Home-Gift Product

**Case:** [FP-003](../cases/FP-003-home-gift-fit.md)  
**Outcome represented:** Proceed  
**Framework version:** Internal pilot v0.1  
**Reviewer:** Synthetic worked example

## 1. Evidence Standard

| Input | Classification | Supports | Does not support |
|---|---|---|---|
| E1: direct and wholesale order exports | Direct Evidence | Observed unit sales in stated channels and period | Universal demand or future sales |
| E2: six openings and four reorders | Direct Evidence | Bounded wholesale acceptance and repeat behavior | Expected reorder rate for Hearthline |
| E3: price and case pack | Verified Fact in current authorized line sheet | Current proposed economics and order unit | Buyer acceptance or final commission |
| E4: packaging and barcode | Direct Evidence from current sample | Current packaging readiness for ordinary gift retail | Every category or Buyer requirement |
| E5: 96% on-time shipment log | Direct Evidence with first-party limitation | Observed Brand reliability at current scale | Reliability at materially higher volume |
| E6: Hearthline assortment and demonstrations | Direct contextual evidence / Strong Proxy for preliminary fit | Credible match thesis | Buyer intent |
| E7: no contact | Verified Fact in case state | No Buyer evidence exists | Rejection or lack of need |

**Additional material inputs**

- “The price structure supports workable Business margin” is an **Assumption** until all relevant costs and Buyer requirements are known.
- “Demonstrability and packaging suit Hearthline” is an **Assumption** supported by a Strong Proxy.
- “Observed reorders will transfer to Hearthline” is an **Assumption** and must not become an **Estimate** without a method.
- No material **Weak Proxy** or **Model-Generated Inference** controls the review.

**Unsupported claims**

- “Hearthline will place an order.”
- “Four reordering accounts prove Hearthline will reorder.”
- “The Brand can scale without limit.”
- “Current Product evidence authorizes outreach.”

**Decision-controlling unknowns**

- representation mandate, commission, territory, conflicts, and authority;
- capacity at proposed volume;
- Hearthline need, timing, economics, and decision process.

## 2. Human Judgment Standard

**AI may assist with**

- reconciling orders and reorders;
- checking line-sheet completeness;
- comparing assortment and price architecture;
- preparing mandate and Buyer-discovery questions.

**Requires human review**

- authenticity and scope of operating records;
- Brand suitability and capacity;
- preliminary Business-fit rationale;
- mandate negotiation and any later outreach.

**Cannot be delegated**

- representation acceptance;
- Product recommendation;
- final fit;
- outreach;
- claims, negotiation, and relationship decisions.

**Representative-owned decision**

Proceed to representation discussions and capacity verification. Buyer outreach remains prohibited until authority exists.

**Specialist review**

No specialist review is indicated by the case facts. It may become necessary if Product, material, insurance, or contract questions arise.

## 3. Placement Cycle

**Current stage:** Qualify.

**Completed entry criteria**

- Product and Brand identity established for pilot purposes;
- direct Product, wholesale, packaging, pricing, and fulfillment evidence exists;
- no stop condition appears;
- a credible future Business-fit hypothesis exists.

**Missing exit criteria**

- mandate and Representative economics;
- territory/conflict verification;
- higher-volume capacity;
- final Brand qualification record.

**Next required action**

Proceed to representation discussion, confirm capacity, mandate, authority, economics, and conflicts. If authorized, create a Target-stage Hearthline record and verify Buyer fit before Prepare.

**Prohibited premature action**

Contacting Hearthline, claiming to represent Alder Finch, or promising reorders.

## 6. Final decision

**Decision:** Proceed  
**Scope:** Proceed to representation discussion and completion of Brand qualification; not Buyer outreach.  
**Rationale:** Direct Product, wholesale, packaging, economics, and current-scale operational evidence supports the next bounded action.  
**Confidence:** Supported.  
**Required next action:** Confirm mandate, commission, territory, conflicts, capacity, and authority.  
**Owner:** Representative  
**Review trigger:** Proposed representation agreement and capacity evidence.

## 7. Pilot observations

- A Proceed decision must always name its bounded scope.
- Small scale does not prevent a supported next action.
- The Placement Cycle should allow a future target hypothesis without changing the active stage to Target.
