# Completed Review — FP-002 Beauty Product Claims

**Case:** [FP-002](../cases/FP-002-beauty-claims.md)  
**Outcome represented:** Do Not Proceed  
**Framework version:** Internal pilot v0.1  
**Reviewer:** Synthetic worked example

## 1. Evidence Standard

| Input | Classification | Supports | Does not support |
|---|---|---|---|
| E1: TikTok views and clicks | Direct Evidence of Brand-reported platform activity | Observed attention and clicks in the stated period | Efficacy, safety, salon fit, wholesale demand |
| E2: 4.6/5 and irritation reports | Direct Evidence of public review content with Limited confidence | Mixed purchaser experience and a risk signal | Clinical effect or irritation rate without denominator and verification |
| E3: “clinically proven” and acne claim | Verified Fact that the Brand makes the claims | Need for substantiation and review | Truth or permissibility of claims |
| E4: chemist reviewed formula | Brand assertion / Weak Proxy | A question to investigate | Qualified claims or labeling approval |
| E5: sample omits cautions seen in reviews | Direct Evidence of current label plus review signal | Potential labeling and communication gap | Definitive legal noncompliance |
| E6: clients ask about acne Products | Direct Evidence of general salon need | Category interest | Fit, safety, or demand for Dermalume |

**Additional material inputs**

- “High link clicks indicate purchase demand” is an **Assumption** based on a **Weak Proxy**.
- “General salon interest transfers to this Product” is an **Assumption**; the Buyer email is at most a **Strong Proxy** for category relevance, not Product fit.
- “A strong retail price supports margin” is an **Assumption** because wholesale terms are missing.
- No material **Estimate** or **Model-Generated Inference** is used in this review.

**Unsupported claims**

- “Clinically proven.”
- “Reverses hormonal acne in seven days.”
- “The chemist review makes the claims acceptable.”
- “TikTok traction proves salon demand.”
- “High average reviews resolve irritation concerns.”

**Decision-controlling unknowns**

- claim substantiation and permitted language;
- qualified labeling/regulatory review;
- safety, irritation, complaint, and return records;
- insurance and Brand issue process;
- exact authorized Buyer-facing claims.

## 2. Human Judgment Standard

**AI may assist with**

- extracting every Product claim from materials;
- comparing label, deck, website, and review language;
- organizing complaint themes;
- preparing a source and missing-evidence register.

**Requires human review**

- source verification;
- risk severity;
- permitted claim set;
- Brand trust and representation suitability;
- Buyer-facing communications.

**Cannot be delegated**

- whether to represent the Brand;
- whether claims are acceptable for use;
- Product recommendation;
- final outreach, salon presentation, ethical decision, or dispute response.

**Representative-owned decision**

The Representative must refuse the current representation and presentation path using the proposed claims.

**Specialist review**

Qualified regulatory/claims and, where appropriate, safety review is required. The specialist determines applicable requirements; AI and the Representative do not.

## 3. Placement Cycle

**Current stage:** Qualify.

**Completed entry criteria**

- preliminary Product and Brand evidence collected;
- material claims, review evidence, and possible Buyer need identified;
- risk signal documented.

**Missing exit criteria**

- supportable claims;
- resolved label and safety questions;
- completed Retail Readiness;
- verified Brand controls and acceptable recommendation basis.

**Next required action**

Stop external progression. Require the Brand to withdraw or substantiate claims, correct materials as required, and obtain appropriate specialist review before any new qualification decision.

**Prohibited premature action**

Accept representation, contact the salon about this Product, repeat acne claims, or use social activity as efficacy evidence.

## 4. Relationship Triangle

| Party | Potential value | Obligations | Risks / warning signs | Condition |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Brand | Salon distribution if Product and claims become supportable | Accurate claims, safe labeling, issue support, fair terms | Current unsupported material claims and missing controls | Unsupported |
| Business Buyer | Possible Product for expressed client category need | Staff must communicate responsibly and manage client trust | Salon could amplify unsupported claims and irritation risk | Unsupported |
| Representative | Possible compensation and salon relationship | Verify claims, refuse misleading advocacy, protect Buyer | Commission would depend on unsafe or unsupported communication | Unsupported |

**Can all three receive legitimate value?** Not through the current proposed path.

## 5. Decision Filter

| Test | Result | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Legitimate Brand value | Fail for current path | Value depends on claims that are not substantiated |
| Legitimate Buyer value | Fail | Buyer cannot make an informed recommendation |
| Representative professionalism | Fail if pursued | Current advocacy would exceed evidence |
| Long-term relationships | Fail | Salon and client trust are exposed |
| Evidence support | Fail | Material claims are unsupported |
| Trust protection | Fail | Risk would be transferred without adequate disclosure/control |
| Efficiency without replacing judgment | Conditional | AI may organize; specialist and human decisions remain essential |
| Alignment with Standard | Fail | Conflicts with honesty, care, evidence, and recommendation duties |
| Public defensibility | Fail | Ryva could not defend repeating the claim from this record |
| Improves profession beyond short-term revenue | Fail | It rewards traction over responsible evidence |

## 6. Final decision

**Decision:** Do Not Proceed  
**Scope:** Current representation, salon outreach, or presentation using the Product and proposed claims.  
**Rationale:** Material unsupported claims and labeling/safety concerns prevent a responsible recommendation.  
**Confidence:** Strong for stopping the current path.  
**Required next action:** Brand-led correction and qualified specialist review before a new case is opened.  
**Owner:** Brand for remediation; Representative for refusal and record.  
**Review trigger:** New substantiation, corrected materials, and specialist disposition.

## 7. Pilot observations

- Do Not Proceed applies to the current path; it need not mean permanent exclusion.
- The Frameworks need a visible specialist-review task and owner.
- Ryva Pro must distinguish “claim observed” from “claim verified.”
