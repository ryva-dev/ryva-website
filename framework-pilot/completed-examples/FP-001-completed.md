# Completed Review — FP-001 Viral Pet Toy

**Case:** [FP-001](../cases/FP-001-viral-pet-toy.md)  
**Outcome represented:** Investigate Further  
**Framework version:** Internal pilot v0.1  
**Reviewer:** Synthetic worked example

## 1. Evidence Standard

| Input | Classification | Supports | Does not support |
|---|---|---|---|
| E1: 1.8 million views screenshot | Direct Evidence of reported platform views, subject to screenshot authenticity; Weak Proxy for wholesale demand | Observed attention on three posts | Verified sales, durable demand, Product quality, pet-store demand |
| E2: 4.7/5 from 1,240 labeled purchasers | Direct Evidence of platform review record; Limited confidence | Observed purchaser sentiment on that platform | Independent Product validation or absence of manipulated reviews |
| E3: praise and 46 charging complaints | Direct Evidence of review content | A recurring issue requiring diligence | Defect rate or safety conclusion without unit and complaint verification |
| E4: $39 price and 14-day return policy | Verified Fact on the Brand website at capture time | Current consumer offer | Wholesale price, margin, returns, or Business terms |
| E5: Paws & Pine assortment and price range | Direct public evidence of current assortment; Strong Proxy for preliminary fit | Category and price compatibility hypothesis | Buyer need, authority, interest, or MOQ acceptance |
| E6: wholesale and operational records unavailable | Unknown for each missing field | Need for diligence | Negative proof that the Brand is incapable |

**Additional material inputs**

- “The consumer price could support wholesale margin” is an **Assumption**.
- “Paws & Pine may value demonstration” is an **Assumption** supported only by preliminary assortment context.
- “Viral attention may transfer to store demand” is an **Assumption** based on a **Weak Proxy**.
- No material **Estimate** or **Model-Generated Inference** is used in this review.

**Unsupported claims**

- “The Product has strong wholesale demand.”
- “Viral attention will drive store sales.”
- “The charging problem is minor.”
- “The $39 price guarantees enough wholesale margin.”
- “Paws & Pine will want the Product.”

**Decision-controlling unknowns**

- Brand identity and authority;
- Product and charging risk documentation;
- wholesale readiness, economics, inventory, fulfillment, and returns;
- verified sales, refund, and acquisition concentration;
- actual Buyer need and authority.

## 2. Human Judgment Standard

**AI may assist with**

- organizing public reviews by reported issue;
- identifying missing wholesale fields;
- comparing public assortment and price ranges;
- preparing diligence questions; and
- summarizing source dates and limitations.

**Requires human review**

- authenticity and representativeness of dashboard and review evidence;
- severity of charging complaints;
- Product and Brand qualification;
- Business-fit hypothesis;
- privacy, source-rights, and category-risk treatment.

**Cannot be delegated**

- decision to recommend the Product;
- acceptance of representation;
- final Buyer-fit decision;
- final outreach;
- claims made to the Buyer; and
- ethical decision to continue or stop.

**Representative-owned decision**

Whether to continue bounded internal diligence. The Representative may not decide that the Brand has passed qualification based on this record.

**Specialist review**

Potential category/product-safety review may be required after specifications and test records are obtained. The current evidence is insufficient to define the exact specialist scope.

## 3. Placement Cycle

**Current stage:** Discover.

**Completed entry criteria**

- legitimate research candidate recorded;
- source and reason for interest captured;
- preliminary Product, Brand, and possible Buyer context identified.

**Missing exit criteria**

- complete research record;
- Product and Brand evidence sufficient for Evaluate;
- disposition of charging concern;
- decision to close or advance.

**Next required action**

Verify Brand identity and request wholesale, Product, safety, sales, returns, inventory, and fulfillment evidence. Do not contact Paws & Pine.

**Prohibited premature action**

Representing the Product, generating a Buyer-facing recommendation, quoting social popularity as demand, or initiating outreach.

## 5. Decision Filter

| Test | Result | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Legitimate Brand value | Conditional | A suitable retailer could create value if readiness exists |
| Legitimate Buyer value | Unknown | Preliminary category fit exists; Product and economics are unverified |
| Representative professionalism | Pass for diligence only | Continuing research is professional; advocacy is not |
| Long-term relationships | Unknown | No wholesale or repeat relationship evidence |
| Evidence support | Conditional | Evidence supports investigation, not recommendation |
| Trust protection | Pass if no outreach occurs | Limits and complaints remain visible |
| Efficiency without replacing judgment | Pass | AI may organize evidence; human owns decision |
| Alignment with Standard | Pass for bounded diligence | Evidence-before-certainty and three-party value are preserved |
| Public defensibility | Pass for investigation; Fail for recommendation | Ryva can defend asking for proof, not a Product claim |
| Improves profession beyond short-term revenue | Pass | Diligence prevents viral metrics from driving poor outreach |

## 6. Final decision

**Decision:** Investigate Further  
**Rationale:** Available evidence supports a plausible research candidate but not Product qualification, Brand qualification, representation, Business fit, or outreach.  
**Confidence:** Supported for the investigation decision; Limited for Product potential.  
**Required next action:** Request and verify the decision-controlling evidence.  
**Owner:** Representative  
**Review trigger:** Receipt of wholesale, Product-risk, sales-quality, inventory, fulfillment, and return evidence.

## 7. Pilot observations

- Reviewers may disagree whether E1 is Direct Evidence or Weak Proxy. Both attributes are useful: it directly records attention and weakly proxies wholesale demand.
- The stage remains Discover even though a possible Buyer is named; naming a Buyer does not establish Target.
- Ryva Pro needs claim-level evidence use, not one evidence label without context.
