# Completed Review — FP-014 Average Product, Strong Buyer

**Case:** [FP-014](../cases/FP-014-average-product-strong-buyer.md)  
**Outcome represented:** Ambiguous; reasonable reviewers may disagree  
**Framework version:** Internal pilot v0.1  
**Reviewer:** Synthetic worked example

## 1. Evidence Standard

| Input | Classification | Supports | Does not support |
|---|---|---|---|
| E1: adequate but undifferentiated sample | Direct Evidence from comparison | Current basic quality and limited differentiation | End-user satisfaction or poor suitability |
| E2: 98% on-time across 63 orders | Direct Evidence with first-party limitation | Observed Brand reliability | Performance for the custom 1,500-unit configuration |

| E6: no custom sample | Unknown for custom quality | Need for validation | Failure of customization |

**Additional material inputs**

- “Functional simplicity creates legitimate value here” is an **Assumption** supported by direct Buyer evidence.
- “Historical reliability transfers to custom production” is an **Assumption** based on a Strong Proxy.
- “A one-time order may create broader relationship value” is an **Assumption**.
- Any future account-value or satisfaction projection would be an **Estimate**; none is used for this decision.
- No material **Weak Proxy** or **Model-Generated Inference** controls the review.

**Unsupported claims**

- “The Product is highly differentiated.”
- “A 98% history guarantees custom delivery.”
- “The Buyer will place the order.”
- “The opening order proves long-term Relationship Value.”
- “Employees will value the kit.”

**Decision-controlling unknowns**

- custom-sample quality and durability;
- final price, margin, commission, returns, damage, and acceptance terms;
- packaging waste result;
- whether adequate Product quality meets the Buyer's final standard.

## 2. Human Judgment Standard

**AI may assist with**

- summarizing the Buyer brief;
- comparing terms, schedules, and sample requirements;
- preparing a validation checklist;
- identifying unsupported Product claims.

**Requires human review**

- adequacy of Product quality for this Buyer purpose;
- sample acceptance criteria;
- three-party economics;
- custom production and delivery risk;
- terms and relationship value.

**Cannot be delegated**

- Product recommendation;
- acceptance of “adequate” quality as suitable;
- claim and presentation choices;
- negotiation, final close, and relationship commitment.

**Representative-owned decision**

Whether the evidence supports a bounded custom-sample step and whether the result is presented as Proceed With Conditions or Investigate Further.

**Specialist review**

No specialist review is inherently required. Packaging or contractual expertise may be used if custom specifications or liability terms warrant it.

## 3. Placement Cycle

**Current stage:** Present.

**Completed entry criteria**

**Missing exit criteria**

## 6. Two reasonable decisions

### Interpretation A: Proceed With Conditions

The authorized Buyer has defined a practical need that does not require novelty. The Product's adequacy may be sufficient in context. Proceed to a custom sample under written acceptance, pricing, waste, quality, and delivery conditions.

**Confidence:** Supported for the sample step.

### Interpretation B: Investigate Further

The missing custom sample is the central evidence required to determine whether the Product creates legitimate Buyer value. Treat the next step as investigation, avoiding “proceed” language that might imply a favorable recommendation.

**Confidence:** Supported for further investigation.

## 7. Resolution evidence

The disagreement should be resolved by:

- custom sample inspected against written Buyer requirements;
- Buyer feedback on utility, quality, presentation, and waste;
- final price, margin, commission, returns, damage, and acceptance terms;
- production test or other evidence supporting custom capacity;
- explicit decision scope in Ryva Pro.

## 8. Final decision and pilot conclusion

**Preferred pilot outcome:** Proceed With Conditions, strictly limited to the custom-sample step.  
**Reasonable alternative:** Investigate Further.  
**Required next action:** Produce and evaluate the custom sample and final commercial terms.  
**Prohibited action:** Place, book, or externally characterize the Opportunity as won.

## 9. Pilot observations

- The distinction between Proceed With Conditions and Investigate Further can be semantic when both prescribe the same bounded next action.
- Ryva Pro should prioritize decision scope and next action over outcome label alone.
- The Frameworks should clarify that Product differentiation is contextual; ordinary usefulness may be legitimate value when the Buyer explicitly prioritizes it.
