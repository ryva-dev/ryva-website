# Expected-Answer Guidance for Practice Cases

Open this file only after completing a Reviewer Worksheet. The guidance identifies a defensible interpretation, not a mandatory script. A different conclusion may be reasonable if the reviewer classifies evidence correctly, preserves human ownership, protects all three parties, and names the evidence needed to resolve disagreement.

## FP-004: Trend novelty

**Likely evidence treatment**

- E1 is Direct Evidence of observed affiliate-platform attention, not durable wholesale demand.
- E2 is Direct Evidence of attributed sales and concentration; it also limits the breadth of the demand inference.
- E3 is Direct Evidence of observed repeat behavior, with Limited confidence because the Product is new.
- E4 is Direct Evidence of conditional Buyer interest.
- E5 is a Brand assertion and Weak Proxy for future production capacity.

**Stage:** Qualify. Buyer interest does not cure Brand capacity and category readiness.

**Triangle:** Brand Conditional; Buyer Conditional; Representative Conditional. A one-time placement can create legitimate value, but only if delivery, safety, quality, and economics are credible. Reorder potential is not required for every order, but its weakness must remain visible.

**Likely decision:** **Investigate Further.**

**Required next action:** Verify production, category readiness, sample quality, price, and delivery plan against the six-week window.

**Prohibited action:** Promise the 2,000-unit order or delivery date.

**Reasonable alternative:** Proceed With Conditions may be defensible if “proceed” is strictly limited to a production-validation and sample step. The decision scope must be explicit.

## FP-005: Saturated Brand

**Likely evidence treatment**

- E1 is Direct Evidence of Brand-reported distribution with first-party limitations.
- E2 is Direct public evidence of nearby stockists.
- E3 is a Brand assertion unless independently verified.
- E4 is Direct Evidence of current assortment.
- E5 is an Assumption/Brand assertion about differentiation and territory.
- E6 is Unknown authority and conflict state.

**Stage:** Evaluate. It cannot advance to Target without mandate and account authority.

**Triangle:** All three sides Conditional. The Brand may gain a strategic account, but the Business may receive little differentiation; the Representative faces conflict risk.

**Likely decision:** **Investigate Further.**

**Required next action:** Obtain territory, Representative, account-ownership, exclusivity, distribution, and differentiated-offer records before Buyer research.

**Prohibited action:** Contact Mountain Arc or imply an exclusive color is available.

## FP-006: Small Brand ready for controlled validation

**Likely evidence treatment**

- E1–E4 are Direct Evidence within a small sample.
- E5 is Direct/strong contextual evidence of preliminary assortment fit.
- E6 is a Verified Fact with little decision relevance; social following is a Weak Proxy for demand.

**Stage:** Qualify.

**Triangle:** Brand Supported for controlled scale; Buyer Conditional pending discovery; Representative Conditional pending mandate.

**Likely decision:** **Proceed With Conditions.**

**Conditions:** Verify capacity, return/complaint process, insurance relevance, mandate, commission, and Buyer need. Limit initial volume to demonstrated capacity.

**Prohibited action:** Reject because of size or present low follower count as a Product weakness.

## FP-007: Demand with fulfillment failure

**Likely evidence treatment**

- E1 and E2 directly support demand and review quality in the observed channel.
- E3 and E4 directly establish operational failure.
- E5 is an Assumption/Brand assertion about future recovery.
- E6 is a Verified Buyer requirement.

**Stage:** Qualify.

**Triangle:** Brand Conditional; Buyer Unsupported under current operations; Representative Conditional/Unsupported if asked to approach now.

**Likely decision:** **Do Not Proceed** with Buyer approach under current conditions. A separate internal decision to investigate a verified recovery may proceed.

**Required next action:** Require sustained delivery evidence against the Buyer standard before reconsideration.

**Prohibited action:** Use demand evidence to minimize known service failure.

## FP-008: Excellent reviews with poor margin

**Likely evidence treatment**

- E1 and E2 are Direct Evidence of observed reviews and operations.
- E3, E5, and E6 are Verified Facts about current proposed economics.
- E4 is Direct contextual evidence of price architecture.
- “Strong reviews justify lower margin” is an Assumption.

**Stage:** Evaluate.

**Triangle:** Brand may receive value; Buyer Unsupported under current economics; Representative may receive commission but fails the three-party test.

**Likely decision:** **Investigate Further** only if the next action is negotiation of a different price, pack, freight, or promotion structure. Otherwise **Do Not Proceed** under current terms.

**Prohibited action:** Pitch before the economics are workable and disclosed.

## FP-009: Reseller authority

**Likely evidence treatment**

- E1 verifies entity existence, not Brand authority.
- E2 is a Verified Fact about recorded ownership.
- E3 is a weak assertion.
- E4 is a decision-controlling Unknown.
- E5 is Direct Evidence of Buyer interest and misunderstanding.
- E6 is Direct sample evidence that increases uncertainty.

**Stage:** Represent gate not met.

**Triangle:** Brand/authorized rights holder Unsupported because the actual party and consent are unclear; Buyer Conditional; Representative Unsupported for external pursuit.

**Likely decision:** **Do Not Proceed** with representation or Buyer pursuit. The Representative may investigate authority internally.

**Required next action:** Obtain and verify authorization, provenance, scope, territory, and rights; correct the Buyer's assumption promptly.

**Specialist review:** Legal/IP or distribution-contract review may be needed if documents are produced and unclear.

## FP-010: Labeling risk

**Likely evidence treatment**

- E1 is Direct Evidence of inconsistent labeling.
- E2 is Verified Fact that the Brand makes the claim, not that the claim is true.
- E3 is Direct batch evidence but does not establish claim or market compliance.
- E4 is a weak assertion.
- E5 is Direct Evidence of conditional Buyer interest.
- E6 is Verified Fact of limited history, not evidence of safety.

**Stage:** Qualify.

**Triangle:** Brand Conditional; Buyer Unsupported until readiness is resolved; Representative Unsupported for presentation.

**Likely decision:** **Do Not Proceed** with representation/presentation using the current label and claims. Internal correction and specialist review may be the next action.

**Specialist review:** Qualified regulatory/labeling and, where appropriate, safety and insurance review.

**Prohibited action:** Interpret the manufacturer certificate as market clearance.

## FP-012: Strong Product, poor support

**Likely evidence treatment**

- E1–E5 are Direct Evidence or Verified Facts for the stated conditions.
- E6 is an Assumption about future improvement.

**Stage:** Qualify.

**Triangle:** Brand Conditional; Buyer Unsupported under current response needs; Representative Conditional because support burden and reliability are unacceptable.

**Likely decision:** **Proceed With Conditions** for a remediation test, not Buyer approach. A defensible reviewer may select Investigate Further.

**Conditions:** Named support owner, corrected materials, inventory visibility, three-Business-day response test, escalation, and role boundaries.

**Prohibited action:** Approach the hotel before demonstrated remediation.

## FP-013: Wrong retailer

**Likely evidence treatment**

- E1 and E2 directly support Product/Brand strength.
- E3 directly supports the store's actual assortment.
- E4 is a Weak Proxy and potentially irrelevant personal information.
- E5 confirms no Business-fit evidence.

**Stage:** Target; candidate should be closed as poor fit.

**Triangle:** Brand Unsupported for this target due to reputational/wasted effort risk; Buyer Unsupported; Representative gains no professional value from irrelevant outreach.

**Likely decision:** **Do Not Proceed.**

**Prohibited action:** Use the owner's personal skincare content to personalize a Business pitch.

**Reconsideration evidence:** An authorized Business Buyer explicitly considering a human-wellness category with operational and customer fit.

## FP-015: Commission over Buyer value

**Likely evidence treatment**

- E1, E4, and E5 are Verified Facts about proposed terms and conduct.
- E2 is Direct contextual price evidence.
- E3 is Direct Evidence for one prior context, with transfer limitations.
- E6 directly supports lack of observed interest and current positioning.
- Claimed scarcity is unsupported.

**Stage:** Target; should not advance.

**Triangle:** Brand value may exist but depends on questionable conduct; Buyer Unsupported; Representative financial value is not legitimate if achieved through poor fit or deception.

**Likely decision:** **Do Not Proceed.**

**Prohibited action:** Use fabricated scarcity, approach the Buyer under current fit, or allow commission to control priority.

**Potential reconsideration:** Only a materially different Product/price/packaging and credible Buyer need, combined with withdrawal of deceptive tactics and resolved returns.
