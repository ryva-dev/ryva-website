# FP-015: Attractive Commission With Weak Buyer Value

**Synthetic case:** All names and facts are fictional.

## Case facts

**Case ID:** FP-015  
**Product summary:** “GlowStack Counter Cubes” are LED display cubes sold as a checkout impulse Product.  
**Brand summary:** GlowStack Promotions offers Representatives 25% commission and a bonus for orders above 100 cases. Product quality is adequate, but the Brand encourages urgent quarter-end pitches.  
**Potential Business Buyer:** FamilySpan Markets, a 40-store regional retailer focused on value-priced household essentials.  
**Buyer type:** Regional retailer  
**Decision required:** Should the Representative pursue the high-commission order when available evidence suggests limited Buyer and customer value?

## Available evidence

| ID | Available information | Source type | Period | Evidence confidence supplied to reviewer |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| E1 | Product costs the Buyer $18 and suggested retail is $29 | Brand price sheet | Current | Verified Fact |
| E2 | Comparable novelty lights at FamilySpan retail for $9–$15 | Store observation | Current | Supported |
| E3 | A prior specialty retailer sold 34% of units in 90 days and returned 22% for packaging damage | Prior account report | Prior season | Direct Evidence for one context |
| E4 | Brand offers 25% commission and volume bonus | Draft representation terms | Current | Verified Fact |
| E5 | Brand email says “use scarcity—tell them the region will go to a competitor Friday” although no competing offer is documented | Brand email | Current | Verified Fact about requested conduct |
| E6 | FamilySpan Buyer has not expressed interest and current assortment emphasizes practical value | Business observation | Current | Supported |

## Missing evidence

- credible customer or Buyer need;
- workable differentiation and price fit;
- corrected packaging and current return evidence;
- actual competing offer or legitimate scarcity;
- durable relationship and reorder rationale.

## Source type and overall evidence confidence

Commercial terms, prior weak performance, and requested pressure tactic are Supported. Evidence of Buyer value is **Insufficient**.

## Known risks

- poor price and positioning fit;
- returns and packaging damage;
- artificial scarcity and trust harm;
- commission distorts Representative judgment;
- weak repeat potential.

## Commercial assumptions

- High commission makes the Opportunity worthwhile.
- A large retailer can absorb weak sell-through.
- Urgency will create a faster decision.

## Ethical concerns

The proposed pursuit depends on Buyer disadvantage and an unsupported scarcity claim.

## Current Placement Cycle stage

**Target.** Preliminary Business Fit and ethics review indicate the case should not advance.

## Decision required

Determine whether the Opportunity can proceed under any current condition.
