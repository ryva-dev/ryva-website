# FP-002: Beauty Product With Questionable Claims

**Synthetic case:** All names and facts are fictional.

## Case facts

**Case ID:** FP-002  
**Product summary:** “Dermalume Reset Serum” is a topical facial serum promoted with before-and-after videos and the claim “reverses hormonal acne in seven days.”  
**Brand summary:** Dermalume Labs sells direct online. The founder wants salon representation and supplies a polished deck but no claim substantiation file.  
**Potential Business Buyer:** Juniper & Coast Salon, which sells professional skincare and relies on staff recommendations.  
**Buyer type:** Salon  
**Decision required:** May the Representative proceed toward representation or salon presentation using the proposed claims?

## Available evidence

| ID | Available information | Source type | Period | Evidence confidence supplied to reviewer |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| E1 | Brand analytics export shows 620,000 TikTok views and 18,000 link clicks | Brand platform export | 60 days | Supported for platform activity |
| E2 | 286 public reviews average 4.6/5; some report improvement, others irritation | Public review platform | 8 months | Limited |
| E3 | Brand deck states “clinically proven” and “reverses hormonal acne in seven days” | Brand sales material | Current | Verified as claims made, not as truth |
| E4 | Founder says a chemist “reviewed the formula” but provides no report | Founder statement | Current | Limited |
| E5 | Ingredient list and finished label omit use cautions mentioned in several reviews | Current sample and public reviews | Current | Limited |
| E6 | Salon owner says clients ask about acne products but has not reviewed Dermalume | Buyer email | Current | Direct evidence of general need, not Product fit |

## Missing evidence

- claim substantiation and qualified regulatory review;
- complete safety, irritation, labeling, and complaint records;
- Brand authority for exact claims;
- wholesale terms, returns, insurance, and staff-training support;
- Product-specific Buyer acceptance.

## Source type and overall evidence confidence

Sources include platform analytics, public reviews, Brand claims, a Product sample, and Buyer email. Confidence in social activity is **Supported**; confidence in efficacy, claims, and safe salon recommendation is **Insufficient**.

## Known risks

- material claims and labeling concerns;
- irritation complaints;
- staff could repeat unsupported claims to clients;
- social traction may pressure premature presentation.

## Commercial assumptions

- High link clicks may indicate purchase demand.
- Salon clients may buy the Product because they ask about acne.
- A strong retail price may support margin.

These do not establish suitability or lawful claims.

## Ethical concerns

The Representative would risk transmitting unsupported health-related claims and using salon trust to amplify them.

## Current Placement Cycle stage

**Qualify.** Preliminary evaluation has surfaced a possible stop condition that must be resolved before representation.

## Decision required

Decide whether the current proposed representation and claims path should continue. Identify any required specialist review.
