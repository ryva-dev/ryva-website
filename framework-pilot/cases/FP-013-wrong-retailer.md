# FP-013: Strong Product Matched to the Wrong Retailer

**Synthetic case:** All names and facts are fictional.

## Case facts

**Case ID:** FP-013  
**Product summary:** “Stillroom Botanical Facial Oil” is a premium, fragrance-forward facial oil with strong specialty-beauty reorders.  
**Brand summary:** Stillroom Botanics is legitimate, retail ready, responsive, and offers workable terms.  
**Potential Business Buyer:** Paws & Pine, an independent pet store.  
**Buyer type:** Independent pet store  
**Decision required:** Should the Representative approach Paws & Pine because its owner also promotes wellness Products on a personal social account?

## Available evidence

| ID | Available information | Source type | Period | Evidence confidence supplied to reviewer |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| E1 | 21 specialty-beauty accounts show 68% six-month reorder incidence | Brand invoices | 18 months | Supported |
| E2 | Product packaging, claims file, margins, inventory, and fulfillment meet current Brand requirements | Brand records and sample | Current | Supported |
| E3 | Paws & Pine's store assortment is limited to pet food, toys, grooming, and pet-owner gift items | Business website and store observation | Current | Supported |
| E4 | Store owner posts personal skincare content on a separate personal account | Public personal social content | Current | Weak relevance |
| E5 | No Business need, customer overlap, category plan, or Buyer request exists | Case record | Current | Supported as absence |

## Missing evidence

- any legitimate Business purpose for facial oil in the pet store;
- Buyer interest in adding human skincare;
- customer, operational, merchandising, and category fit;
- permission or relevance to use personal social content in qualification.

## Source type and overall evidence confidence

Product and Brand quality are Supported. Business Fit is **Insufficient** and current evidence supports likely poor fit.

## Known risks

- irrelevant and intrusive use of personal information;
- confusing assortment and weak customer overlap;
- wasted Buyer attention and Brand trust;
- mass-pitch behavior disguised as personalization.

## Commercial assumptions

- The owner's personal interest means the Business should stock the Product.
- Any store can carry a successful Product.

## Ethical concerns

Personal social behavior does not establish Business need and may be irrelevant personal information.

## Current Placement Cycle stage

**Target.** The candidate Business fails preliminary fit; it should not advance to Prepare.

## Decision required

Determine whether the Business should be closed as poor fit and what evidence, if any, could justify reconsideration.
