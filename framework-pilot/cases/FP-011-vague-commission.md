# FP-011: Brand With Vague Commission Terms

**Synthetic case:** All names and facts are fictional.

## Case facts

**Case ID:** FP-011  
**Product summary:** “Morrow Carry Trays” are stackable serving trays with credible wholesale history and retail-ready packaging.  
**Brand summary:** Morrow Form wants independent Representatives but says commission will be “around 12% depending on the account” and has no written calculation, payment, return, or account-attribution rule.  
**Potential Business Buyer:** Slate & Stem, a lifestyle boutique with tabletop and hosting categories.  
**Buyer type:** Boutique  
**Decision required:** May the Representative proceed with Brand discussions and target preparation while commission terms are unresolved?

## Available evidence

| ID | Available information | Source type | Period | Evidence confidence supplied to reviewer |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| E1 | 14 wholesale accounts and 9 reorders are supported by redacted invoices | Brand invoice records | 18 months | Supported |
| E2 | Packaging, barcode, line sheet, MOQ, lead times, and returns are current and complete | Samples and Brand documents | Current | Supported |
| E3 | Shipment records show 94% on-time performance | Brand operational export | 12 months | Supported with first-party limitation |
| E4 | Founder email says commission is “around 12%,” paid “after things settle,” with exceptions for larger accounts | Founder email | Current | Verified Fact about vague proposal |
| E5 | Slate & Stem assortment and price architecture appear compatible | Business website and visit notes | Current | Supported fit hypothesis |
| E6 | No representation agreement or Buyer contact exists | Case record | Current | Supported |

## Missing evidence

- exact commission rate, basis, exclusions, adjustments, payment trigger and timing;
- account attribution, territory, house accounts, returns, cancellations, post-termination, and dispute rules;
- representation authority;
- Buyer need, timing, and decision process.

## Source type and overall evidence confidence

Product, operations, and preliminary Business-fit evidence is **Supported**. Representative economics and mandate authority are **Insufficient**.

## Known risks

- commission dispute or unpaid work;
- unclear account ownership;
- premature outreach;
- Product strength may cause the Representative to accept vague terms.

## Commercial assumptions

- The founder intends fair compensation.
- The commission ambiguity can be resolved before external activity.
- Slate & Stem is likely to fit.

## Ethical concerns

The Representative must not imply authority or commit work before terms are clear, but need not reject a credible Brand solely because its first draft is incomplete.

## Current Placement Cycle stage

**Represent.** Product and Brand qualification support mandate discussion; the representation gate is not complete.

## Decision required

Decide whether and under which conditions internal representation negotiation and target research may continue.
