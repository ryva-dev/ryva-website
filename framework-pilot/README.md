# Ryva Framework Pilot Pack

**Version:** Internal pilot v0.1  
**Framework owner:** Founder  
**Final approver:** Founder  
**Status:** Ready for controlled internal pilot  
**Public use:** Prohibited

The Framework Pilot Pack tests whether five foundational Ryva Frameworks are clear, consistent, usable, and sufficient to guide the first usable version of Ryva Pro:

1. [The Ryva Evidence Standard](../ryva-frameworks/01-core/06-evidence-standard.md)
2. [The Ryva Human Judgment Standard](../ryva-frameworks/01-core/07-human-judgment-standard.md)
3. [The Ryva Decision Filter](../ryva-frameworks/01-core/04-decision-filter.md)
4. [The Ryva Placement Cycle](../ryva-frameworks/01-core/01-placement-cycle.md)
5. [The Ryva Relationship Triangle](../ryva-frameworks/01-core/02-relationship-triangle.md)

The pilot does not validate numerical scoring and must not be used to support public claims, certification decisions, automated outreach, or production ranking.

## Pilot design

- Fifteen synthetic cases cover different Products, Brands, Business Buyers, evidence quality, commercial conditions, and risks.
- Five cases include fully completed reviews.
- Ten cases provide blank practice reviews, with expected-answer guidance stored separately.
- One review should take approximately 20–30 minutes.
- Reviewers record confusion, disagreement, missing rules, and data burden rather than forcing consensus.
- Specialist review is requested only when a case genuinely raises legal, financial, regulatory, safety, or category-specific questions.

## Start here

1. Read [Pilot Instructions](pilot-instructions.md).
2. Select a case from the [Case Register](cases/README.md).
3. Complete the [Reviewer Worksheet](reviewer-worksheet.md).
4. Use the [Evidence Record Template](evidence-record-template.md) and [Decision Log Template](decision-log-template.md) as needed.
5. Compare practice cases with [Expected-Answer Guidance](cases/expected-answer-guidance.md) only after finishing.
6. Record results in the [Pilot Results Template](pilot-results-template.md).
7. Record proposed framework changes in the [Framework Revision Log](framework-revision-log.md).

## Operating rules

- Case names and facts are synthetic. They are not claims about real companies.
- Available evidence is limited to what the case provides. Reviewers may identify evidence to obtain but must not invent it.
- “Unknown” is not average evidence.
- AI may help organize case facts; it may not make the final recommendation, Buyer-fit, ethical, representation, outreach, or relationship decision.
- The weakest unsupported side of the Relationship Triangle can prevent progression.
- A Decision Filter outcome must name the next action.
- Framework changes require a revision-log entry and Founder approval.

## Outputs

The pilot should determine:

- where reviewers agree or diverge;
- which evidence labels are misunderstood;
- which missing facts control decisions;
- whether the Placement Cycle produces the correct next action;
- whether the Relationship Triangle prevents commission-first decisions;
- whether human responsibility remains meaningful;
- whether the Decision Filter produces actionable outcomes;
- which rules conflict or duplicate one another;
- which data is feasible for Ryva Pro; and
- whether the Frameworks are ready for scoring design.

The initial [MVP Data Model](mvp-data-model.md) is deliberately smaller than an enterprise CRM architecture.

Initial design conclusions and recommended revisions are consolidated in [Initial Findings and Recommendations](initial-findings-and-recommendations.md).
